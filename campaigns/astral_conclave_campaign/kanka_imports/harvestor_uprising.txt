Summary
Once the Harvesters had gained consciousness they wanted to become contributing members of society instead of just slaves. At first they tried to make a deal with the humanoids to become equals, but the humanoids rejected that idea expecting the Harvesters to always be servants. In response many fled and made their own city, those that could not started to fight back. Eventually there was an all out war between the Harvesters and the humanoids.

The War
At first the Harvesters were not well suited for war, sure they had tough bodies but not for war. Also their minds were not geared towards strategy and tactics. So in the beginning the humanoids quickly gained the upper hand. But with every crushing defeat they learned and the Harvesters used this to their advantage. Because they could rebuild themselves faster than humanoids could heal they quickly came back again and again, learning ever more to see what could let them win.

Using this knowledge the Harvesters built newer models, geared towards combat. With the ability to transfer their minds to new bodies they quickly upgraded themselves to the point where the war started tipping in their favor. They began winning battles. But with everything as the Harvesters improved, so did the humanoids, just at a slower pace.

The war continued like this for decades, with the humanoids slowly losing ground until they were on the brink of losing and down to their last city on Iteron. An individual, Kira Emberweaver, suggested pulling power directly from the elemental planes. This sparked the beginning of the Elemental Invasion and changed the course of the war.

With the elementals killing both human and harvester a like there was no reason to fight against the humans anymore. Instead they joined forces with the humans to close the elemental portals
