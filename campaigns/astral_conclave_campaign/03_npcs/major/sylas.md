# Sylas

## Role
Messenger of the gods and agent of fate focused on preserving world balance.

Sylas exists at the edge of prophecy, divine command, and temporal fracture. He has access to future possibilities, alternate timelines, or all timelines at once; the exact truth is unclear even to those who encounter him.

## Motivation
He sees a great calamity approaching and believes the players are entangled in the threads leading south. He is not yet certain whether they will prevent the disaster or cause it.

Sylas serves gods who believe humanity and mortal civilization must be kept from advancing too far too quickly. In past ages, mortal advancement drew the attention of the Others: an interdimensional species seeking to conquer this dimension and all of its planes. The gods cannot directly interfere, so they use agents like Sylas to nudge events, create friction, prevent dangerous breakthroughs, and, when necessary, facilitate wars or collapses that keep civilization below the threshold the Others can easily find.

After the last cycle allowed humanity to advance too far, the gods began trying a new tactic with Sylas. Rather than only sending him with messages, they exposed him to visions of possible futures or made him live across many timelines at once. Whether he is seeing the future, remembering other failed timelines, or existing in several outcomes simultaneously remains unresolved.

## Traits
Calm, watchful, indirect, unsettlingly patient.

## Limits
- Sylas is not allowed to freely reveal future information.
- He cannot give direct instructions that would let mortals bypass meaningful choice.
- He can warn through metaphor, timing, questions, dreams, or carefully chosen omissions.
- He can place people near the right door, but he cannot tell them exactly what waits behind it.
- If he violates these limits, divine law, timeline backlash, or the attention of the Others may punish him and the people he is trying to protect.

## Companion
A familiar owl that can guide others to him.

## Connections
- Helped [[Orion]]
- Watches the players from a distance
- Serves the gods as an indirect messenger and temporal pressure valve
- May understand more about the Others than any mortal ally
- May be testing whether the players can prevent calamity without being handed the answer

## The Others
The Others are an interdimensional species or civilization that notices dimensions whose inhabitants become advanced enough to disturb, pierce, or signal across planar boundaries. Their goal is conquest across dimensions and planes.

The gods' harsh interventions are partly defensive: they believe mortal suffering inside a contained world is preferable to allowing the Others to find and conquer all planes connected to it.

## Current Relevance
The [[Astral Conclave]]'s asteroid-pull device, crystal harvesting, obedience technology, and planar experimentation risk pushing the world back toward the same threshold that once attracted the Others.

Sylas may not oppose every act of suppression the gods have caused, but he is increasingly uncertain whether managed collapse can still protect the world. The players may represent a third path: stopping the Conclave without letting divine fear turn into endless sabotage of mortal progress.
