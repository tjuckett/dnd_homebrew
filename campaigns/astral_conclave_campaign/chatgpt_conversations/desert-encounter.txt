if players are moving at 60ft per round, how do they gain against the mobile fortress

If both the PCs and the fortress only move 60 ft/round in a straight line, they don’t gain. Relative distance stays flat. So you need at least one of these in the rules, or the chase doesn’t work.

Here are clean ways to make it work (pick 1–3):

1) PCs are faster than the fortress
Fortress: 60 ft/round (as written)

Desert bikes: 90–120 ft/round baseline (and can “dash”)

Vehicle Dash: If the rider uses their action to Dash, bike speed doubles for the round.
Result: they close fast, but give up attacks/spells.

2) The battlefield is “scrolling” relative to the fortress
Make the fortress the frame of reference:

On lair action, the fortress advances 60 ft (terrain scrolls)

PCs must spend movement to not fall behind.
So:

If a rider moves < 60 ft in their turn, they effectively lose distance.

If they move > 60 ft, they gain.

This makes 60 the “treadmill speed” and creates tension.

3) Add “Position Bands” instead of feet
Use bands like:

Far (can’t attack)

Near (ranged attacks)

Alongside (boardable)

On top
Each round:

Beat the fortress “pace DC” with a control check to move 1 band closer.

Fail = lose 1 band (or stay).

This avoids the math.

4) Let terrain create openings
Even at equal speed, PCs can gain if the fortress is forced to:

slow (rocks, turns, drill collisions)

swerve (loses speed)

stop (sabotage gyros/engine)
So give mechanical triggers like:

If the fortress turns this round: speed becomes 45 next lair action.

If drill is used: speed becomes 30 next lair action.

If harpoon hits something heavy: speed becomes 45 next lair action.

5) Slipstream / Boost actions
Give bikes a repeatable boost:

Bonus Action: Boost (recharge 5–6 or limited uses)

Gain +30 ft this round, but on a 1–2 the bike takes 1d10 damage.

The simplest fix
Set bike speed to 100 ft/round and let riders Dash for 200 ft/round (giving up attacks). Fortress stays 60. Now it’s a real choice: close distance or fight.

To keep it a true chase and prevent everyone from boarding immediately, the key is to separate speed from boarding access. Even if the bikes can keep up, getting onto the fortress should require positioning, timing, and risk.

Here’s a system that works well at the table and stays simple.

Core Chase Structure
Treat the chase as distance bands behind the fortress, not raw feet.

Band	Distance	What You Can Do
4 – Far	120+ ft	Only long-range attacks
3 – Closing	60–120 ft	Ranged attacks, skill checks
2 – Flank	30–60 ft	Harpoon can hit you, drill danger
1 – Boarding Range	0–30 ft	Attempt boarding
0 – On Fortress	—	Inside fight begins
Players start in Band 3 or 4.

Bike Speed vs Fortress
Fortress speed: 60 ft (lair action movement)

Bike speed: 80 ft

So players only gain ~20 ft per round if they stay safe.

But rocks, turns, and attacks will often cancel that gain.

Closing the Distance
At the end of their turn, riders can attempt to close distance.

Control Check:
Dexterity + Land Vehicles (or Acrobatics)

Result	Effect
18+	Move 2 bands closer
13–17	Move 1 band closer
12 or less	No progress
Fail by 5+	Lose 1 band
This represents navigating rocks and terrain.

Why Everyone Can’t Board Early
You must meet ALL conditions to board:

Be in Band 1

Use Action to attempt boarding

Pass a DC 16 Athletics or Acrobatics

Survive defensive reactions

Fortress Anti-Boarding Defenses
When someone attempts boarding, roll 1d4:

d4	Defense
1	Steam Vent – DC 15 Con save or take 2d8 fire
2	Turret Fire – +7 attack, 2d8 force
3	Harpoon Sweep – DC 15 Dex save or pulled off bike
4	Grinding Treads – DC 15 Dex or take 2d10 damage
So boarding is dangerous.

The One Player Who Jumps Early
A daring player might try this stunt:

Jump the Fortress
Requirements:

Must be Band 2

Must move at least 60 ft this turn

Check: DC 18 Acrobatics

Success:

Land on the rear car.

Failure:

3d6 damage and fall behind Band 3.

This lets a bold player board early without everyone doing it.

Fortress Turns Matter
You asked earlier how far it can turn.

Rule:

Each lair action the fortress may shift 1 column (15 ft) left or right.

Effect:

Riders in that column must make DC 15 control checks

Failure:

2d10 damage

Lose 1 distance band

This is the main way the fortress maintains distance.

Rocks Slow Players More Than the Fortress
Rocks only threaten bikes.

If a bike hits a rock:

DC 15 control

Fail:

3d8 damage

Lose 1 band

This means players rarely gain distance every round.

Expected Timeline for Level 10 Party
Typical flow:

Round	Event
1	players dodging rocks
2	one player attempts jump
3	party reaching Band 2
4	first successful boarding
