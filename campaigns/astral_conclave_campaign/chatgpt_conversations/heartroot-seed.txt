🌱 The Heartroot Seed
Wondrous Item, Rare (requires attunement by a druid, ranger, or bard)

Item Description:
The Heartroot Seed is a small, pulsating acorn-like relic wrapped in faintly glowing roots that seem alive, shifting gently as if searching for soil. When attuned, its bearer feels a faint heartbeat in sync with their own.

Powers:

Entangling Growths: While attuned, you can cast Entangle at will without expending a spell slot.

Fey Bloom (1/long rest): As an action, you can activate the Seed, causing it to emit fey energy in a 5-foot radius for 1 minute. Allies in the radius regain 1d4 hit points at the start of their turn as vines of luminous ivy wrap protectively around them.

🌳 Lore
Origins: The Heartroot Seed was said to be grown from the World-Tree of Lirael, a primordial oak at the center of the Feywild where the roots of all forests converge. During an ancient war between the Summer and Winter Courts, druids of the Concord harvested a single root infused with the tree’s lifeblood, shaping it into a Seed of renewal and binding.

Purpose: It was created not as a weapon, but as a balm for war, meant to heal wounds and bind warriors together. To hold the Heartroot Seed is to carry a fragment of the Feywild’s heartbeat.

Current Status: Few Seeds remain. They are coveted by druids, fey nobles, and even warlords who wish to claim its restorative magic. To the Fey, it is more than an artifact — it is a sacred relic of balance, and many would do anything to reclaim or protect it.

🧚 The Fey and the Heartroot Seed
The Verdant Circle (Summer Fey): A druidic order of Fey sworn to nurture the Heartroot’s scattered fragments. They see its healing powers as a gift meant to restore harmony, and may approach the bearer as an ally — or judge them unworthy and demand its return.

The Thorns of Winter (Winter Fey): This militant faction views the Seed as a weakness — they believe it disrupts the natural culling of life in winter. They might attempt to destroy or corrupt it, turning its healing aura into one that drains vitality instead.

The Seed-Tenders: Mischievous lesser Fey (tiny dryad-like sprites with roots for hair and glowing leaf-wings) are sometimes drawn to the Seed. They whisper warnings, offer cryptic guidance, or play tricks to “test” whether the bearer is worthy of carrying it.

🌌 Adventure Hooks
The Withering Grove: A grove in the Feywild is dying because its Heartroot Seed was stolen. The Fey beg the adventurers to recover it before the corruption spreads.

The Seed’s Call: The Seed pulses at night, pointing toward another Seed hidden deep in a cursed forest. Collecting them all may awaken the Heartroot Tree itself.

Courtly Intrigue: Both the Summer and Winter Courts send emissaries to claim the Seed, each offering vastly different rewards. The players must decide who, if anyone, to trust.
