🐉 Why the Hydra Uses the Shipwreck as a Lair
1. Shelter & Territory
The shipwreck is a massive, stable structure on the ocean floor, creating a cavern-like den.

The metal hull and collapsed decks form protective chambers where the hydra can coil and rest.

Unlike caves, the wreck draws prey in naturally — smaller fish hide in its corridors, and scavengers feed on its decay, creating a steady food chain.

2. Energy Source
The wreck’s dormant reactor core or void-tainted machinery leaks energy. The hydra, mutated over centuries, thrives in its aura.

This explains its cold/lightning resistance and why it became stronger than a normal hydra.

3. Treasure & Territory Marking
The ship still contains shiny metals, arcane salvage, and corpses. Hydra heads are animalistic and greedy — they hoard wreckage and corpses in their lair.

For you: this makes the wreck a natural treasure trove of salvage parts, so the players must explore it for Kira’s EMP components.

⚔️ Ensuring There’s a Fight
Hydras are apex predators — they won’t just let intruders sneak in and out. Here’s how you guarantee combat while still giving it narrative weight:

1. Location Design
The parts Kira needs are deep inside the wreck — near or even beneath the hydra’s resting chamber.

That way, even if players try to be stealthy, they must pass right through its lair.

Place salvage in glowing void-containment crates lodged under the hydra’s coils — forcing the party to confront it.

2. Hydra Instincts
Hydras are territorial. It will attack immediately if it senses intruders in its lair, even if it’s not hungry.

Each head acts independently; if even one notices, it screeches and the others awaken.

Framing: “As you pry the reactor casing free, the ship creaks. Then… bubbles rise around you. From the shadows, five sets of glowing eyes open at once.”

3. Narrative Trigger
The shipwreck’s reactor is unstable. When the players disturb it (pulling salvage, activating old systems), the hydra interprets this as an intrusion and awakens.

You could even have the lair react — currents swirl, the wreck shudders, and the hydra emerges.

4. Safety Nets
If you’re worried the players might try to flee:

Kira tells them outright: “The parts you need are inside the wreck… but something massive is nesting there. You won’t be able to get them unless you kill it.”

The hydra’s lair actions (currents, collapsing wreckage, eel swarms) make retreat very difficult. Once the fight starts, the wreck itself “locks them in” narratively until it’s over.

🎭 Framing the Encounter
To make it memorable:

Build dread before the fight — show broken warforged, shredded giant crabs, huge gnawed beams of metal.

Start with atmosphere — as they salvage parts, describe the oppressive silence, then the ship groaning… followed by many eyes opening.

Force confrontation — put what they need under the hydra, or make it so the hydra is drawn to their salvage attempt.

Yes — even if the old facility is mostly stripped and decomissioned, you can still make scavenging rewarding but thematic by mixing atmosphere, lore, and a few practical resources. Since this was a pre-war underwater research station, here’s what players might realistically find:

🧭 Scavenging in Kira’s Underwater Lab
1. Practical Salvage (Functional but Modest)
Corroded Tools & Gear

Rusted diving harnesses, cracked glass helmets, broken welding rigs. Mostly junk, but clever players may improvise uses.

Arcane Capacitors (Low Power)

Fist-sized crystals that once powered instruments. Most are drained, but a few still hold a charge — enough to serve as one-use spell batteries (e.g., cast Lightning Bolt, Gust of Wind, or Wall of Water once).

Purification Filters

Old water filtration tubes still partly functional. Could be salvaged into a portable water purifier or used by Kira to improve her living systems.

2. Environmental Hazards Turned Loot
Flooded Wings

Some wings are completely filled with water, clogged with algae and barnacles. Searching them may reveal bioluminescent growths that can be harvested into potions or light sources.

Pressure Leaks

A cracked bulkhead with high-pressure currents may conceal a hidden storage closet wedged shut. Breaking through could yield salvage but risks flooding the chamber.

3. Lore & Worldbuilding Finds
Research Journals (Waterlogged but Restorable)

Logs describing experiments on sea elementals and engineered sea creatures, hinting at what once lived here.

May include cryptic warnings about the ancient sea serpent.

Broken Construct Frames

Hulking metal skeletons of worker drones, inert for decades. Nothing combat-ready, but scavenging parts might give Kira scrap to tinker with (flavor reward: next time she outfits the party, she has “upgraded gear”).

Old Security Archives

A cracked crystal console may still play fragmented images of the war — soldiers, elementals, or void machines breaching the lab. Could foreshadow Conclave tactics.

4. Rare/Flavorful Treasures
Void-Tainted Coral

Coral that has absorbed trace void energy, still faintly glowing. Can be sold as a curio, fashioned into a charm, or used as a minor spell component.

Pressure-Forged Pearls

Rare pearls formed in the deep, worth some gold, but mostly flavor loot.

Pre-War Insignia

Badges, seals, or tattered uniforms from the original researchers. Great for story clues if the party digs into history.

🎭 How to Run It
Make it atmospheric scavenging: most rooms are collapsed, flooded, or empty. The feeling should be eerie, like wandering a sunken ruin.

Reward exploration: let curious players uncover small consumables, lore, and crafting materials, but not high-value loot.

Tie it back to Kira: anything useful (scrap parts, capacitors, filters) can be repurposed by her into improved gadgets later.
