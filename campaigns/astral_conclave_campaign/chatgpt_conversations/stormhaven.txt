Stormhaven is a thriving coastal town nestled along the temperate shores of a vast ocean, surrounded by dense forests and rugged cliffs that give it a natural defensive advantage. Its strategic location makes it a key trading hub, with merchants stopping to trade goods from across the seas and surrounding regions. This natural wealth, coupled with its defensible position, made it an attractive prize for the Shadow Fangs.

History and Control by the Shadow Fangs
Originally a peaceful fishing village, Stormhaven began to prosper due to its location on a prominent maritime trade route. Its modest port grew into a bustling harbor, drawing merchants, adventurers, and opportunists. However, as the town flourished, its defenses remained rudimentary, leaving it vulnerable to raids.

The Shadow Fangs, an infamous band of thieves and raiders, initially targeted Stormhaven as another plunder site. But when they witnessed its wealth and potential, their leader, a cunning strategist named Kaelric "The Veil," proposed a bolder plan: occupation. The Fangs launched a decisive assault, quickly overwhelming the local militia and taking control of the town. Instead of pillaging it and leaving, they established themselves as rulers, using their cunning and brute force to maintain order and extract wealth from the town's commerce.

Agreement with the Astral Conclave
The Shadow Fangs' control of Stormhaven caught the attention of the Astral Conclave, a mysterious and powerful organization that manipulates planar magic. The Conclave approached Kaelric with a proposition: they would install a portal in Stormhaven, allowing them access to this strategic location for their own purposes. In return, they would provide the Fangs with magical assistance, intelligence on potential threats, and a degree of legitimacy in the eyes of powerful entities who might otherwise oppose their rule.

The portal itself is a well-guarded secret, hidden beneath the town in a cavern accessible only to those with permission from both the Fangs and the Conclave. Its presence has solidified the Fangs' power, as it ensures their access to otherworldly resources and deterrence against outside forces.

General Description of Stormhaven
Stormhaven is a paradox of thriving trade and underlying lawlessness. The Shadow Fangs have imposed their own order: merchants are allowed to trade freely, but they must pay hefty "taxes" to the Fangs. Those who refuse or attempt to undermine their authority face swift and brutal consequences. Despite this, the town remains bustling, as the Fangs' protection ensures relative safety from external threats like pirates or rival raiders.

The town's architecture reflects its dual nature. The central harbor is alive with activity, filled with ships unloading exotic goods, while the surrounding areas show signs of neglect, with crumbling buildings and shadows that conceal the Fangs' watchful enforcers. At the heart of the town stands the repurposed mayoral mansion, now Kaelric's headquarters, a symbol of the Shadow Fangs' dominance.

Stormhaven's populace is diverse, consisting of merchants, sailors, refugees, and even some adventurers drawn by rumors of the portal or the Fangs' hidden treasures. The presence of the Astral Conclave adds an enigmatic air, with cloaked figures occasionally seen moving through the streets, sparking whispers and unease among the townsfolk.

Stormhaven: A Revised Overview
Stormhaven is a smaller, run-down coastal town in a temperate region, situated along an important but somewhat neglected trade route. The town shows signs of past prosperity, but years of neglect and Shadow Fang rule have left it worn and crumbling. Despite its diminished state, its strategic location and connections to powerful groups make it a place of intrigue and danger.

Shadow Fang Control
The Shadow Fangs, under the leadership of the cunning Kaelric "The Veil," saw in Stormhaven a perfect opportunity to establish a base of operations. Initially looters and raiders, they recognized the value of controlling a stable foothold. They moved in, taking control with little resistance and solidifying their rule through calculated intimidation and strategic alliances.

Though small, the town serves as the Shadow Fangs' hidden stronghold. They maintain a loose grip on the townsfolk, allowing commerce to continue while ensuring their own dominance. The Fangs take a significant cut from any trade or ventures, keeping the town in a state of perpetual dependency and decay.

Agreement with the Astral Conclave
A pivotal factor in the Shadow Fangs' continued power is their arrangement with the Astral Conclave, a shadowy organization with an interest in planar magic and pre-war technology. The Conclave provided the Fangs with advanced pre-war technology, including weapons, tools, and devices of arcane and mechanical origin, bolstering their influence and operational capability.

In return, the Conclave maintains a portal in Stormhaven, hidden in the town’s depths. This portal serves their mysterious purposes, but it also enables the Conclave to covertly hire mercenaries for their operations. These mercenaries, unknowingly connected to the Shadow Fangs, are hired for missions through intermediaries and sent through the portal, never aware of the Fangs' overarching influence.

The Conclave reserves the right to call upon the Shadow Fangs for direct assistance when needed, creating a subtle but powerful relationship where both sides benefit. The advanced technology provided by the Conclave helps the Fangs maintain their dominance over Stormhaven and furthers their goals in the wider world.

Description and Atmosphere
Stormhaven is a town marked by faded glory and opportunistic survival. Its cobbled streets are cracked, its buildings patched and leaning. The harbor, once a hub of activity, is functional but dilapidated, with fewer ships than in its heyday. Signs of pre-war prosperity, such as ruined towers and remnants of arcane infrastructure, are scattered throughout, serving as a reminder of what was lost and what the Conclave may be seeking to rediscover.

The townsfolk are hardy, accustomed to the Shadow Fangs' rule and the town's decayed state. Some dream of revival, while others are resigned to their circumstances. A sense of unease pervades the streets, as rumors of the Conclave's activities and the mysterious disappearances of some who delve too deeply into the wrong business add an air of quiet paranoia.

Traits: Secretive, pragmatic, and coldly loyal to the Conclave’s goals.

Traits: Brutal, fiercely loyal to Kaelric, and a master tactician in combat.

Lysara Greywind
Role: Informant and Spy

Traits: Quick-witted, resourceful, and skilled in both combat and deception.

Pulse Gauntlets: Mechanical gloves that emit powerful shockwaves, used to break through doors or incapacitate foes.

Kinetic Shields: Personal, wrist-mounted devices that can project small energy barriers, deflecting attacks.

Arcane Implants: Some enforcers have undergone augmentations, such as enhanced reflexes or low-light vision, giving them an edge in combat and intimidation.

Twist: The player may learn that their family’s death was not random but orchestrated as part of a larger plot connected to the Conclave’s plans.

Uncovering the Portal’s Purpose

Complication: The portal is guarded by powerful constructs and wards, requiring cunning and strength to breach.

The Conclave’s Hidden Agenda

Escalation: The Conclave’s true intentions might involve opening a dangerous planar rift, threatening Stormhaven itself.

The Lost Relic
Overview: A piece of pre-war technology stolen from the Conclave ends up in the player’s hands. This relic is highly sought after by both the Fangs and the Conclave, forcing the player into a web of intrigue and danger.

Potential: The relic could have ties to the player’s backstory, such as being a link to the family killed by the Shadow Fangs.

The Rebellion
Overview: A faction of townsfolk secretly plans to overthrow the Shadow Fangs. They enlist the players’ help, but the rebellion is woefully under-equipped to deal with the Fangs' enforcers.

Choice: The players must decide whether to support the rebellion or exploit the chaos for their own gain.

Detailed Plot Hooks for Stormhaven
1. The Rebellion
Overview:
A group of disillusioned townsfolk and former mercenaries have grown tired of the Shadow Fangs’ oppressive rule and their alliance with the Astral Conclave. The rebellion seeks to overthrow the Fangs and restore Stormhaven’s independence, but they lack the resources to combat the enforcers’ advanced weaponry and the Conclave’s influence.

Key Players:

Aedric Torne: A grizzled ex-mercenary who lost his family to Shadow Fang extortion. He serves as the rebellion’s leader, determined but haunted by his past failures.

Lyra Fenwick: A resourceful smuggler and spy who acts as the rebellion’s connection to the outside world. She provides intelligence on the Fangs’ operations and the Conclave’s activities.

Joren Thatch: A blacksmith skilled at reverse-engineering pre-war technology. His inventions are crude but effective, offering the rebellion a fighting chance.

Events:

Recruitment: The rebellion approaches the players for help. They may offer information, safe haven, or even the chance for the player seeking revenge to face their family’s killers.

Sabotage: The rebellion plans coordinated strikes on key targets, such as the enforcers’ weapon caches or the portal’s power source. The players can assist or lead these efforts.

Internal Betrayal: One of the rebellion’s members is secretly working for the Fangs or the Conclave, feeding information back to Kaelric or Isera Duskfall. The players must uncover the traitor before the rebellion is destroyed.

Climactic Moment:
A full-scale assault on the Shadow Fangs’ headquarters erupts, with the players either leading the charge or playing a critical role. The rebellion’s success hinges on neutralizing Derrik "Stonehand" Veynor and his enforcers, armed with their pre-war tech.

2. The Conclave’s Hidden Experiments
Overview:
The Astral Conclave has been using Stormhaven as a covert testing ground for pre-war technology and augmentations. The Shadow Fangs’ enforcers are among their test subjects, but townsfolk have also been subjected to their experiments under the guise of providing "medical aid" or "economic relief."

Key Experiments:

Augmented Enforcers: Some Shadow Fang enforcers possess advanced implants or grafts, such as enhanced strength, sensory upgrades, or built-in weaponry.

Control Devices: A subset of the populace unknowingly carries experimental neural implants, allowing the Conclave to monitor their movements and emotions—or even control their actions in emergencies.

Technological Hazards: Malfunctioning devices have caused strange phenomena in the town, such as localized temporal distortions, harmful radiation, or even minor planar breaches.

Events:

Unusual Disappearances: Townsfolk are vanishing without explanation. Investigations reveal that the Conclave has been abducting individuals to use as test subjects.

The Test Subject: The players encounter a rogue enforcer or townsfolk who escaped the Conclave’s experiments. They bear horrific scars and advanced technology, serving as a living example of the Conclave’s cruelty.

Uncovering the Laboratory: The Conclave’s hidden lab beneath Stormhaven becomes a focal point of the story. Breaking in requires cunning, as it is heavily guarded by constructs and wards.

Climactic Moment:
The players discover the Conclave’s ultimate goal: to perfect a device that manipulates planar boundaries. If activated, it could tear open a rift that threatens to engulf Stormhaven. The players must stop the experiment while dealing with both the Conclave’s operatives and the Shadow Fangs, who see the device as their own key to power.

Connecting the Hooks
The rebellion and the Conclave’s experiments naturally intersect:

Common Enemy: The rebellion learns of the Conclave’s experiments and aims to destroy their lab as part of their uprising.

Player Choice: The players might prioritize aiding the rebellion or focusing on the Conclave’s machinations, forcing difficult choices that shape the town’s future.

Moral Complexity: Some townsfolk benefiting from the Conclave’s "aid" might resist the rebellion, complicating the players’ decisions.

Key Locations in Stormhaven
1. The Docks

Key Features:

Dockmaster’s Office: A shabby structure where the dockmaster oversees trade and "taxes" levied by the Shadow Fangs.

Hidden Smuggler’s Coves: Caves beneath the docks house illicit goods and serve as secret meeting spots for the Shadow Fangs and rebellion.

Description: The repurposed mayoral mansion stands atop a hill overlooking the town. Its imposing structure is a mix of grandeur and decay, with banners of the Shadow Fangs hanging from its weathered walls.

Key Features:

Kaelric’s Chambers: Lavish quarters filled with pre-war relics and maps detailing the Fangs’ operations.

War Room: A central planning area with holographic projectors provided by the Conclave, used to plot raids and enforce control.

Dungeon: Holding cells for captives, including rebellious townsfolk and those who cross the Fangs.

3. The Portal Caverns
Description: Beneath the town lies a hidden cavern where the Astral Conclave’s portal is housed. The air hums with arcane energy, and glowing glyphs illuminate the chamber.

Key Features:

The Portal: A swirling, otherworldly gateway that crackles with planar energy.

Conclave’s Laboratory: Adjacent to the portal, this lab is filled with strange machinery, glowing vials, and half-finished constructs.

4. The Rusted Anchor Inn
Description: A run-down tavern near the docks, popular among sailors, merchants, and mercenaries. Its cracked sign depicts a heavily rusted anchor.

Proprietor: Marla Vintyr, a tough-as-nails human woman with a wooden leg and a sharp tongue.

Features:

Common Room: A smoky, noisy space where patrons trade stories, gamble, and drown their sorrows.

Private Rooms: Small and barely clean, but secure. Often used for clandestine meetings.

5. Joren’s Forge
Description: A small smithy at the edge of the town run by Joren Thatch, the rebellion’s blacksmith. The forge is a cluttered mix of traditional tools and salvaged pre-war tech.

Features:

Custom weapons and armor crafted with a blend of iron and scavenged tech.

Hidden compartment beneath the forge used to store rebellion supplies.

6. The Broken Compass
Description: A general goods store with a leaning façade and dusty windows. Its shelves are filled with basic supplies, though some items are clearly scavenged from wrecks or ruins.

Proprietor: Harlon Greaves, an elderly half-elf with a penchant for storytelling and a secret disdain for the Shadow Fangs.

Features:

Adventuring gear, mundane items, and occasional relics of dubious origin.

A backroom accessible only to trusted customers, used by the rebellion.

7. The Temple of Tides
Description: A crumbling temple dedicated to a sea deity, now a refuge for the downtrodden. The Shadow Fangs mostly ignore it.

Caretaker: Sister Elira, a devout cleric who aids the sick and hungry. She secretly supports the rebellion.

Features:

A quiet sanctuary for those seeking solace or healing.

An ancient altar with inscriptions that hint at pre-war maritime magic.

8. The Market Square
Description: Once a vibrant hub, the market square now struggles under the Fangs’ oppressive taxes. Many stalls are abandoned, leaving only the most desperate or resourceful merchants.

Features:

A mix of exotic goods brought by passing traders and local wares.

Occasional black-market stalls offering forbidden or rare items.

9. Lysara’s Den
Description: A nondescript building on the outskirts of town, serving as Lysara Greywind’s base of operations. It’s a hub for espionage and intelligence gathering.

Features:

A concealed meeting room where Lysara plans spy missions.

A network of hidden tunnels leading to key locations around the town.

10. The Wreckyard
Description: A junkyard near the harbor filled with broken ships, wagons, and machinery. It serves as a scavenging ground for both the townsfolk and the rebellion.

Proprietor: Torgen Craghewn, a gruff dwarf with a knack for spotting valuable scrap.

Features:

Salvageable pre-war tech hidden among the debris.

Secret entrances to the rebellion’s hideouts.

Adventure Opportunities
Hidden Tunnels: Exploring the network of secret passages connecting the Portal Caverns, Lysara’s Den, and other key locations.

Mysterious Relic: A forgotten pre-war device lies buried in the Wreckyard, sought by both the Conclave and the rebellion.

Temple Secrets: Ancient inscriptions in the Temple of Tides point to a submerged pre-war vault, tied to the Conclave’s experiments.

Above the town, on a small hill overlooking the bay, looms the Shadow Fang Citadel—a once-proud mayoral mansion now defaced and fortified. Its walls are reinforced with salvaged iron plating, and strange glyphs glow faintly at its perimeter, likely an ominous gift from the Astral Conclave.

The Astral Conclave’s Experiments and Their Dark Purpose
The Astral Conclave’s experiments on captured individuals are not mere cruelty; they serve a greater, secretive purpose tied to their ultimate pursuit of arcane supremacy and control over planar forces. The Conclave believes that unlocking the hidden potential of the mortal body and mind will grant them unparalleled dominance over both the material and extraplanar realms.

Why Does the Astral Conclave Need to Experiment on People?
Perfecting Planar Infusions

The Conclave seeks to merge mortals with planar energies, making them resistant—or even immune—to the chaotic forces of teleportation, extradimensional shifts, and void corruption.

Experiments involve exposing captives to controlled bursts of planar energy, including the Shadowfell’s draining aura, the Feywild’s mutating magic, and Astral plane-induced time distortions.

Desired Outcome: Creating planar-adapted soldiers and agents who can survive in environments that would kill normal beings.

Testing Pre-War Arcane Technology

Many pre-war devices contain arcane augmentation technology that only functions when synced with a living host. The Conclave uses prisoners to test these devices—often with lethal consequences.

Examples of experiments include:

Runic Implants – Embedded sigils that attempt to enhance intelligence, strength, or spellcasting ability—but often result in brain hemorrhages or insanity.

Soul Engines – Arcane machinery that siphons life energy to power ancient war machines or Conclave rituals.

Mind-Control and Memory Extraction

The Conclave wants to develop methods of absolute mental control, ensuring perfectly obedient enforcers and spies who cannot resist their influence.

Experimentation includes:

Implanting sigils of obedience, which erode free will and force compliance.

Memory alteration tests, where captives are subjected to illusions that replace their real memories with fabricated loyalty to the Conclave.

Using extractor crystals, which pull knowledge from captives' minds to uncover hidden secrets, lost locations, or military strategies.

Creating Magic-Resistant Soldiers

The Conclave is researching ways to harden the mortal body against magic, making individuals resistant to spells that would normally incapacitate or dominate them.

Methods include:

Injecting victims with anti-magic essence, often harvested from rare magical creatures.

Forcing prolonged exposure to null-magic zones, seeing who survives the best without arcane energy.

Experimenting with resonance grafts, augmenting captives’ nervous systems to reflect certain spell effects back at their casters.

Artificial Awakening of Dormant Magic

Some people carry latent magical potential but never awaken it naturally. The Conclave is trying to force this process artificially, creating powerful spellcasters from non-magical captives.

Their methods include:

Subjecting captives to magical overloads, exposing them to pure ley-line energy.

Infusing non-mages with elemental cores, attempting to create hybrid arcane warriors.

Injecting harvested draconic or celestial blood into test subjects to awaken potential sorcery—but with horrific side effects.

The Horrors of the Experiments: What the Players Might Witness
Failed Experiments

Bodies with burned-out sigils, their life essence drained away.

Prisoners driven mad from forced planar exposure, muttering about realities overlapping or things moving in the darkness.

Shadow Fang Patrol Patterns in Stormhaven
The Shadow Fangs control Stormhaven with an organized yet unpredictable patrol pattern, ensuring dominance over the town while keeping the populace in a state of fear and uncertainty. Their patrols are strategically designed to protect their interests while allowing illicit activities to flourish under their control.

General Patrol Overview
Squad Size: Most patrols consist of 3-5 enforcers, with stronger patrols near important locations.

Patrol Frequency: Standard routes rotate every 2-3 hours, but additional patrols appear at random intervals to prevent predictability.

Reporting: Each patrol reports back to a central command post (such as the Shadow Fang Citadel or a hidden safehouse) every 4-6 hours unless something urgent arises.

Patrol Types and Routes
1. Dockside Watch (Heavy Presence at Night)
Purpose: Oversee contraband shipments, intimidate merchants, and prevent unsanctioned smuggling.

Pattern:

Two-man teams circulate the main docks every 30 minutes.

Larger patrols (5-6 enforcers) inspect cargo warehouses and check for rival smugglers.

Random stop-and-search operations occur every few hours.

2. Market Square Patrol (Daylight Enforcement)
Purpose: Maintain control over the market, ensure merchants pay their “taxes”, and root out potential rebellion activity.

Pattern:

A slow-moving patrol of 3-4 enforcers walks the square every hour.

One Shadow Fang agent in disguise listens for rumors and watches for potential troublemakers.

Surprise inspections are conducted once per day, targeting specific merchants.

3. Lower Town Slum Patrol (Night-Only Patrols)
Purpose: Suppress any unapproved crime, locate rebels or deserters, and ensure fear remains high.

Pattern:

Smaller squads (2-3 enforcers) patrol the slums every 1-2 hours.

They move erratically, sometimes skipping entire streets before doubling back.

No fixed routes to keep the poor guessing.

4. Ruined Church & Hidden Auction Security (Elite Enforcer Presence)
Purpose: Guard the slave auctions and protect Shadow Fang dealings with the Astral Conclave.

Pattern:

At least 5 enforcers stationed near the ruins at all times.

A roaming patrol of 3 enforcers walks the outer perimeter every hour.

Conclave-aligned agents occasionally reinforce security when high-value trades occur.

5. Shadow Fang Citadel Patrol (Fortified Headquarters)
Purpose: Defend the Citadel, relay patrol reports, and protect Kaelric 'The Veil' and his lieutenants.

Pattern:

Two elite enforcers stand guard at all entrances.

Two-man patrols circle the outer wall every 20 minutes.

Roving squads are deployed when disturbances arise elsewhere in the city.

6. Secret Tunnel & Smuggler Routes (Intercepting Rivals & Spies)
Purpose: Prevent outside forces from undermining Shadow Fang control.

Pattern:

Small, silent teams patrol known smuggling tunnels irregularly, waiting for intruders.

Hidden watchposts use trained owls and magical alarms to detect movement in the tunnels.

How the Players Can Exploit Patrols
