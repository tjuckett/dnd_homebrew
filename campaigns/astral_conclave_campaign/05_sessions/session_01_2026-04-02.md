# Session 01 - April 2, 2026

## Current Situation
- The party already accepted [[Magistra Pyra-IX]]'s mission.
- They reached High Spine Mine, cleared the immediate Heliox threat, and prevented the site from simply collapsing into chaos.
- During the aftermath, [[The Calyxis Reclaimer]] escaped with stolen crystal cargo.
- The party boarded the Reclaimer in the desert and is now fighting their way toward the front car to seize control before it reaches Heliox-aligned territory.
- The rear car has already been detached during the battle.
- The Heliox crew now intends to release the second car as well and flee with only the Reclaimer's engine section if they cannot hold the full vehicle.

## Session Purpose
- Keep the energy of an ongoing moving battle instead of resetting into briefing mode.
- Make each car of the Reclaimer feel like a different tactical problem.
- Force meaningful choices between speed, control, and survival.
- Let Heliox remain morally coherent even while acting as the enemy in this moment.
- End with the party either taking the Reclaimer, crashing it, or choosing what kind of victory they can live with.

## What The Players Are Doing Right Now
- They are already aboard the fortress-train.
- Their immediate objective is to reach the front control chamber before the second car is cut loose.
- They are under pressure from moving terrain, fortress systems, Heliox crew resistance, and the risk of losing both the engine and the stolen crystals in separate directions.
- The longer they take, the greater the chance the Reclaimer escapes as a stripped-down engine section while the cargo car is abandoned, sabotaged, or left behind as a trap.

## Reclaimer Structure
### Rear Car - Already Detached
- The boarding car is gone.
- That separation should matter emotionally and tactically: anyone left behind is now off the main objective, and the party can feel the train getting smaller and more desperate.
- Use the loss of the rear car as proof that the Heliox crew is willing to sacrifice parts of the Reclaimer to preserve the engine and command core.

### Middle Car - Crystal Cargo and Sever Point
- This is now the most important contested space until the front chamber is reached.
- It contains secured stolen crystal loads, stabilization braces, and the coupling the Heliox crew intends to release.
- The party should feel the stakes here: if they fail to stop the detachment, they may save the cargo but lose the engine, or stop the engine but lose the proof and prize of the heist.
- This is the best place for the party to realize they cannot simply destroy everything without handing Heliox a partial success.

### Front Car - Control Chamber and Steering Systems
- This is the real objective.
- The front section contains steering gyros, engine routing controls, drill coordination, and whatever station is needed to change direction or slow the Reclaimer.
- Heliox's most disciplined defenders should anchor here because once this compartment falls, the encounter changes from survival to command.

## Suggested Encounter Flow
### 1. After The First Detachment
- Start with the reality that the rear car is already gone.
- Re-establish who made it onto the surviving cars, what resources have been spent, and whether anyone important was separated.
- Let the players feel that Heliox has already made one ruthless but disciplined call and is fully willing to do it again.

### 2. Push Into the Middle Car
- This should be the hardest attrition point.
- The party has to choose whether to move fast, secure the crystal cargo, rescue anyone trapped, or stop the crew from releasing the second car.
- Give Heliox defenders a clear delaying goal: hold the choke point long enough for the sever mechanism to engage and the engine section to pull away cleanly.

### 3. Sever Or Save Moment
- Add a problem that is not solved by damage alone.
- Good options:
- A crew member is on the coupling release mechanism and only needs one more round.
- A cargo brace is failing, so a violent stop or uncontrolled detachment could rupture the crystal load.
- A stabilizer is about to overload and make the middle car too dangerous to hold.
- The party may have to choose between securing the cargo car and chasing the engine section.

### 4. Final Breach Into the Front Car
- Once the party reaches the front, the encounter becomes a command fight.
- The defenders here should feel more disciplined and mission-focused than the rest of the crew.
- If the party hesitates, the driver or overseer attempts the final disengage, a sharp turn, collision line, drill burst, or lockout sequence.

### 5. Seizing Control
- Gaining the chamber is not the same as instantly mastering the vehicle.
- The party may need to:
- incapacitate the current controller,
- keep the engine stable for a round or two,
- pass checks to understand the controls,
- or decide whether to stop, redirect, or intentionally strand the engine section after the cars split.

## Heliox Goals
- Preserve the crystal cargo if possible, but preserve the engine and command section first.
- Keep Calyxis from reclaiming both the freight and the propaganda victory.
- Prevent outsiders from gaining a usable Heliox war machine.
- If defeat is certain, split the train, deny a clean capture, and escape with the smallest viable section.

## Good Mid-Encounter Revelations
- The crew are not raiders for profit; they believe this seizure is necessary to stop Calyxis from burning out Asmar's future.
- The stolen crystal load includes unusually depleted or unstable material, implying the energy crisis is worse than either city admits.
- The Reclaimer crew has standing orders that mention not just the mine, but a broader preservation campaign.
- The detachments are pre-planned emergency doctrine, which means Heliox expected this raid might become a sacrificial delaying action.
- A control interface or older subsystem briefly reacts to the construct PC in a way Heliox did not expect.

## Strong Enemies For Each Phase
- Rear car: lighter defenders, boarding denial troops, and one hazard-focused specialist.
- Middle car: a tougher holding force backed by a cargo-control enemy or suppression unit.
- Front car: the most competent commander or engine overseer, plus anyone needed to run claws, steering, or harpoon systems.

## Things To Track Behind The Screen
- How many rounds until the second car is released
- How many rounds until the engine section reaches relative safety
- Whether the crystal cargo remains intact
- Which vehicle systems are still active
- Whether the party is splitting up across multiple cars
- Whether any Heliox officer is trying to destroy evidence or deny capture

## Consequences By Outcome
### Party Takes Full Control
- [[Calyxis Forge]] gains a major political and military win.
- Heliox will treat the party as direct participants in the conflict, not neutral outsiders.
- Pyra-IX gains leverage, but that also increases fear that the party is tilting the balance of power.

### Party Saves The Cargo But Loses The Engine
- Calyxis recovers material value and proof of the raid, but Heliox preserves its elite strike asset.
- Heliox can frame the outcome as an acceptable sacrifice under preservation doctrine.
- The party gets a partial victory that still leaves a dangerous enemy mobile in the desert.

### Party Takes The Engine But Loses The Cargo Car
- The party wins the dramatic command fight, but the stolen crystals are destroyed, scattered, or unrecoverable.
- Pyra-IX values the denial of Heliox escape, though the economic cost remains painful.
- This outcome creates arguments inside Calyxis about whether the party protected lives, strategy, or pride.

### Party Stops the Reclaimer but Wrecks It
- The cargo may be partly recovered, but the symbolic victory is messier.
- Heliox can argue outsiders escalated the conflict into destructive chaos.
- Calyxis still values the result, but less cleanly.

### Party Saves Themselves but Loses the Train
- Heliox gains both material and narrative advantage.
- Pyra-IX remains pragmatic, but trust in the party becomes more conditional.
- The next step becomes pursuit, retaliation, or political containment.

## Best Ending Beats
- The party reaches the controls just in time to prevent or interrupt the second detachment.
- A dying or captured Heliox officer delivers a sharp, sincere warning that Calyxis is mistaking extraction for survival.
- The construct PC touches an older interface in the control chamber and the machine responds with impossible recognition.
- The engine escapes or is captured, but the crystals or control logs reveal that Asmar's energy problem is even worse than expected.
