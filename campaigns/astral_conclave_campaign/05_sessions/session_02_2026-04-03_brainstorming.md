# Session 02 Brainstorming - April 3, 2026

## Current Snapshot
- The party captured [[The Calyxis Reclaimer]] and reconnected it to the caboose.
- They now have `8` warforged who had been under outside control and collapsed once the control effect was broken.
- The coordinating device was destroyed, and one affected warforged had also been carrying a second `command bolt`.
- The party is operating the train while waiting for a Calyxis representative to arrive by bike.

## Best Explanation For The Controlled Warforged
- The controlled warforged did not freely choose sabotage. They were forced into it by a final Conclave denial protocol once capture became likely.
- That denial protocol compelled them to destroy the command device so the party could not cleanly trace the control network back to its base.
- One affected warforged also carried a second `command bolt` that worked as a relay key bolt: not a spare control unit, but a keyed authentication component for the next stage of the network.
- That relay key bolt was destroyed under the same denial protocol so the players would be left with recognizable wreckage, but no easy route back to the hidden relay or base.
- Once the device and relay key bolt were broken, the command lattice collapsed violently and scorched recent memory tied to the control state.
- The warforged are not blank shells, but damaged survivors:
- they remember who they were before,
- they do not remember the controlled period,
- and they may have fragments, stress responses, or isolated sensory flashes later.

## Core Tension
- The party won the battle, but now holds a military prize, political leverage, and evidence that something deeper than Heliox aggression is happening.
- The next session should focus on what they do with that win before Calyxis, Heliox, or the hidden network can control the story.

## Recommended Session Spine
### 1. Secure The Train
- Handle damage, the unconscious warforged, and the wreckage of the destroyed `command bolt`.
- Give the party a chance to inspect the ruined control device, the shattered relay key bolt, and any remaining logs or hardware.

### 2. Calyxis Arrives
- A Calyxis representative reaches the train and immediately wants control of the cargo, the recovered vehicle, and the witnesses.
- This creates the first pressure point between cooperation and caution.

### 3. The Warforged Wake
- At least one freed warforged wakes confused and missing the controlled period.
- They can confirm violation and lost time, but not provide a clean explanation.

### 4. First Real Clue
- The party finds evidence that points beyond a simple Heliox raid.
- Best options:
- unusual relay routing,
- impossible command timing,
- or a reaction from the construct PC to older system architecture.

### 5. End On A Choice
- Return to [[Calyxis Forge]] and manage the political fallout.
- Quietly hold back key evidence.
- Chase the signal or bolt signature before it goes cold.

## Best Immediate Questions
- Who were the `8` warforged before they were controlled?
- Why was one of them carrying a second `command bolt`, and what was it keyed to?
- How much does Calyxis get told right away?
- Does the construct PC recognize anything in the recovered systems?

## Good Reveals
- The warforged were overridden, not willing collaborators.
- The control system had a denial protocol designed to destroy traceable evidence through its own thralls.
- The second `command bolt` was a relay key bolt, not a spare control unit.
- The destroyed bolt still matters as evidence even though it no longer functions.
- The Reclaimer may have been acting on signal support older than current city systems.

## How The Party Can Track The Conclave
- Study the wreckage of the command device and relay key bolt for unusual materials, build patterns, and signs of older infrastructure integration.
- Question the freed warforged about where they were before losing time, who last assigned them, and whether any shared memory gaps point to a holding site or relay point.
- Search the Reclaimer's logs and systems for impossible timing, hidden relay acknowledgments, or routing traffic that should not exist in normal Heliox channels.
- Look for other recent cases of missing time, unnatural obedience, or suspiciously precise field coordination among Heliox-linked personnel.
- Have the construct PC test recovered hardware or control surfaces for signs of unauthorized use of older Accord pathways.
- Let the investigation point first to a hidden relay node or covert handling site rather than directly to Heliox leadership.

## Best Scale Of The Conspiracy
- Heliox should not be a puppet city.
- Most of [[Heliox Prime]] should still be acting from its own real preservation ideology.
- The [[Astral Conclave]] works better as a limited covert force using a mix of controlled assets, manipulated channels, and a few willing collaborators.
- Best version: only a small number of direct control devices or relay-capable nodes are active on Asmar, enough to be dangerous but not enough to make the whole continent feel fake.

## Good Ending Beats
- A freed warforged says something that proves they lost time under outside control.
- Calyxis or the construct PC identifies the shattered second bolt as relay-authentication hardware.
- A Calyxis official realizes this victory may hide a bigger threat.
- The construct PC senses something in the old network that should not still be active.
