## Best Explanation For The Controlled Warforged
- The controlled warforged did not freely choose sabotage. They were already under outside control before the Reclaimer mission began.
- The control likely took hold inside [[Heliox Prime]], before they ever boarded [[The Calyxis Reclaimer]].
- The `8` affected warforged were working in the same Heliox logistics/relay area when a hidden obedience device activated through their normal communication channels.
- The Reclaimer crew may have been deployed as compromised assets rather than taken over during the battle.
- The device on the Reclaimer is the same kind of obedience device as the one in Heliox. The Heliox device only reaches city-scale because the [[Astral Conclave]] jury-rigged it into the city's internal communication network.
- The obedience device does not decide what people do. It removes refusal and makes warforged receptive.
- A separate hijacked Heliox command relay distributes actual orders through normal civic/military dispatch channels.
- A hidden [[Astral Conclave]] relay base in the desert sent the actual commands to the Reclaimer device while the vehicle was outside Heliox communication range.
- The obedience devices were created late in the war roughly `200` years ago as an attempt to control warforged, but were never openly deployed because they recreated the exact slavery that helped cause the war in the first place.
- The Conclave is using Heliox to steal existing power crystals and mine more because it needs enormous energy reserves to power its device to pull down the asteroid.
- The final Conclave denial protocol only activated once capture became likely.
- That denial protocol compelled them to destroy the command device so the party could not cleanly trace the control network back to its base.
- One affected warforged also carried a second `command bolt` that worked as a relay key bolt: not a spare control unit, but a direct command/feedback node tied to the hidden desert relay.
- The relay key bolt let the desert base receive simple feedback from Rhem-1 and exert tighter control over them than the rest of the crew.
- That relay key bolt was destroyed under the same denial protocol so the players would be left with recognizable wreckage, but no easy route back to the hidden desert relay base.
- Once the device and relay key bolt were broken, the command lattice collapsed violently and scorched recent memory tied to the control state.
- The warforged are not blank shells, but damaged survivors:
- they remember who they were before,
- they do not remember the controlled period,
- and they may have fragments, stress responses, or isolated sensory flashes later.

## Core Tension
- The party won the battle, but now holds a military prize, political leverage, and evidence that something deeper than Heliox aggression is happening.
- This does not mean the [[Astral Conclave]] caused the Heliox/Calyxis conflict. The war still comes from real scarcity, ideology, fear, and old choices made by both cities.
- The Conclave is exploiting the conflict, steering existing hostility and using the fog of war to hide controlled assets.
- Its practical goal in this arc is crystal acquisition: steal Calyxis reserves, keep Heliox mining, and funnel enough power into the Conclave's asteroid-pull device.
- If revealed carefully, the control evidence could give [[Heliox Prime]] and [[Calyxis Forge]] a common enemy without magically healing their political divide.
- The next session should focus on what they do with that win before Calyxis, Heliox, or the hidden network can control the story.

#### How The Heliox Device Worked
- The initial control event happened in [[Heliox Prime]] through a hidden obedience device connected to a logistics/relay annex.
- The device used normal Heliox communication channels as its carrier, but the channel itself was not the source of control.
- When the `8` warforged were in range, the device suppressed their will and made them passive, obedient, and ready to receive orders.
- The device could affect the worksite area, but its field could not reach far beyond the city walls.
- Once the Reclaimer left Heliox, the crew needed an onboard device to keep them in the same obedient state because the city network could no longer carry the field to them.
- Best clue framing: the city channel did not control them; it carried the obedience field into their bodies.

#### Sovereign Network Override Mechanic
Use this if the construct PC tries to interface with the Reclaimer device, the relay key bolt, Sandglass Relay, or later the Heliox network-amplified obedience device.

##### Core Idea
- The Sovereign cannot be controlled normally by the obedience network.
- Their resonance profile can confuse, interrupt, or briefly override the network.
- This does not require them to become a tyrant, but every use tempts the same forbidden logic: controlling warforged "for their own good."

##### When It Can Be Used
- Touching the destroyed Reclaimer device.
- Touching Rhem-1's relay key bolt wreckage.
- Standing inside the active field of a standalone obedience device.
- Interfacing with Sandglass Relay before it is fully packed or purged.
- Later, entering the network-amplified field inside [[Heliox Prime]].

##### Basic Check
- Ability: Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma, player's choice.
- Add proficiency if using relevant tools, arcane training, or construct-specific interface knowledge.
- Suggested DCs:
  - DC 13: broken Reclaimer wreckage or relay key bolt fragment.
  - DC 15: Sandglass Relay while partially active.
  - DC 17: active standalone obedience device.
  - DC 19: network-amplified Heliox field.
  - DC 21+: trying to free a large number of controlled warforged at once.

##### On Success
Choose one effect:
- Sever: Break the obedience link for one controlled warforged or a small nearby group for `10` minutes.
- Mute: Prevent the desert relay from sending new commands through one device for `1` minute.
- Listen: Receive a directional or sensory impression of the current command source.
- Scramble: Give controlled warforged in range a chance to hesitate, repeat an old command, or take no hostile action for one round.
- Mirror: Send a false "stable and compliant" feedback pulse to the relay, buying time before the Conclave realizes something is wrong.
- Unlock Memory: Restore one controlled warforged's pre-control memory fragment without restoring the whole controlled period.

##### On A Strong Success
If the check beats the DC by `5` or more, choose one stronger effect instead:
- Free A Cluster: Break control for up to `8` warforged in the immediate area until they re-enter an obedience field.
- Trace Command Source: Get a clear direction and rough distance to Sandglass Relay or the active command source.
- Lock Out Handler: Prevent one Conclave operator from using the network for `10` minutes.
- Sovereign Priority: Force the device to recognize the Sovereign as higher authority for one command exchange.

##### On Failure
Choose or roll one complication:
- Feedback Pain: The Sovereign takes psychic or lightning damage appropriate to level.
- Memory Spike: The Sovereign receives a painful flash of the war-era debate that created the obedience devices.
- Conclave Ping: The relay notices a Sovereign-level resonance profile touching the network.
- Command Echo: One controlled warforged nearby repeats their last order once before the link falters.
- False Opening: The device appears to yield, but it is actually trying to route the Sovereign's signature back to the relay.

##### Cost Options
Use one cost when the effect is powerful or the scene needs tension:
- The Sovereign gains one level of exhaustion after the scene.
