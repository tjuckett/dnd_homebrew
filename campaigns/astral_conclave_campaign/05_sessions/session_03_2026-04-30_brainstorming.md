## Sandglass Relay
- Name: Sandglass Relay.
- Location: A temporary setup tucked among wind-cut basalt rocks several hours from the Reclaimer's stopped position.
- Surface Cover: A desert survey camp or salvage crew rest point.
- Actual Use: Temporary [[Astral Conclave]] command relay for the Reclaimer operation, using collapsible antenna masts, crystal batteries, and command consoles packed into reinforced travel crates.
- Why It Was Here: It sent actual commands to the controlled warforged while the Reclaimer was outside normal Heliox communication range.
- Why It Is Abandoned Now: The party long rested, giving the Conclave time to pack personnel, purge logs, and move critical components.

## Three Relay Sites
- The desert command system should be a small relay chain, not one isolated camp.
- Total field relays: `3`.
- Purpose: keep the Reclaimer command stream stable across distance, terrain interference, and Heliox/Calyxis signal noise.
- The relays are not full bases. They are temporary field nodes built to translate, amplify, and hand off command traffic between the Reclaimer and Heliox's [[Civic Directive Lattice]].
- This keeps the [[Astral Conclave]] competent: Sandglass Relay was exposed and abandoned, but it was only one node in a small operational chain.

### Relay 1 - Sandglass Relay
- Status: Abandoned and purge-damaged.
- Function: Reclaimer-specific field relay. It handled the live command stream most directly tied to the captured Reclaimer operation.
- What Remains: Melted relay core, scorched camp traces, damaged command slate shard, abandoned clamps, and scorpion-guarded evidence.
- Session Use: This is the site the party physically reaches in Session 03.

### Relay 2 - Cinderhook Relay
- Status: Actively evacuating after the Sandglass survivors report that a relay core was left behind.
- Function: Secondary handoff relay that helped preserve signal range and direction between Sandglass and the Heliox-side command source.
- Why It Briefly Seems Catchable: The Conclave did not begin fully collapsing Cinderhook until Sandglass personnel escaped and reported the abandoned core. That warning came late, so Cinderhook is in emergency teardown when the party first locks onto it.
- Session Use: If Varyn hooks the repaired command-bolt detector into Sandglass's half-detached core, the combined device can point to Cinderhook as the next relay in the chain.
- Expected Beat: While the party travels toward Cinderhook, its signal goes dead. Unlike Sandglass, Cinderhook's crew finishes the teardown cleanly enough that no active core keeps broadcasting.
- DM Note: Cinderhook should feel urgent and optional. It is a chance to chase the cleanup trail, not a required stop before Heliox.

### Relay 3 - Mirrordune Relay
- Status: Still active because it is waiting for one last shipment of charged crystals to leave the leyline nexus by vehicle.
- Function: Final desert-side translator, masking relay, and emergency warning node. It is not only part of the Reclaimer chain; it is protecting a live crystal-charging operation.
- Session Use: This relay explains how the field network could sync with Heliox without putting all Conclave activity in one obvious camp.
- Why It Is Not Already Evacuated: Mirrordune must stay online until the final charged-crystal transfer clears the leyline nexus. Shutting it down early risks stranding the shipment or exposing the nexus operation.
- Fail-Safe: If Mirrordune sees [[The Calyxis Reclaimer]] approaching, the crew triggers a relay-blast failsafe, escapes by portal, and sends a warning pulse to the nexus ordering its operators to destroy their command bolt and stupor device.
- DM Note: Mirrordune is the last field relay before the trail points to the leyline nexus. It should not become bigger than the nexus unless the party chooses to chase it hard.

## Why The Half-Detached Relay Core Was Left Behind
- The relay core was not a portable computer or command archive. It was the signal-transfer anchor that synchronized Sandglass Relay with Heliox's larger command lattice.
- To make the desert broadcast stable, the Conclave bolted the core into basalt and used it as a heat sink and resonance ground.
- When the Conclave abandoned camp, they started unbolting the core and triggered a purge to destroy any clean data it held.
- The purge did not simply overheat the site. It cracked the basalt anchor and vented into a buried thermal/brine pocket beneath the rocks.
- The sudden heat, mineral steam, vibration, and metal-rich brine woke giant scorpions nesting under the basalt shelf.
- The scorpions surfaced before the crew finished extracting the core.
- The Conclave crew cut their losses, fled through the emergency transit route, and left the core half-detached, partly melted, and fused into its mounting.
- After the purge, the core was no longer useful as Conclave hardware. It was only dangerous as evidence, and the Conclave gambled that slagged crystal would look unreadable or be destroyed by the local scavengers.
- This is why Sandglass still had a detectable signal after abandonment: the interrupted, half-detached core kept leaking a relay pulse.
- Future relays should not automatically do the same. If Cinderhook finishes evacuation properly, its signal can go completely dead.

## What The Half-Detached Core Provides
- It stabilizes the repaired command-bolt detector. After extraction or field calibration, the detector stops wavering between desert echoes and can consistently identify relay-to-relay handoff traffic.
- It proves Sandglass Relay was a transfer node, not the original command source. The core's surviving alignment shows it was handing traffic between the Reclaimer command bolt network and another desert relay.
- It preserves the Reclaimer's final command-feedback pulse in slagged crystal. This can confirm the timing of the takeover and show that the Reclaimer was still receiving outside instructions after leaving normal Heliox range.
- It shows the existence of the relay chain. The core has surviving channel scars:
  - one burned-out local channel tied to Sandglass,
  - one fading outbound handoff channel tied to Cinderhook,
  - and a weaker downstream echo that may only resolve into Mirrordune after Cinderhook is checked or goes dark.
- It does not point straight to the [[Civic Directive Lattice]]. The party can infer that the chain ultimately feeds toward Heliox, but Sandglass itself only gives clean relay-to-relay direction.
- It gives the party portable proof. They probably cannot take the whole fused core, but they can remove a crystal vane, brass timing ring, or slagged resonance shard that Varyn can later use as evidence or as a detector upgrade.

## Mirrordune Fail-Safe
- Trigger: Mirrordune spotters or instruments detect [[The Calyxis Reclaimer]] approaching.
- Immediate Action:
  - the final crystal loading is rushed,
  - the relay crew opens an escape portal,
  - the relay-blast failsafe is armed,
  - and a warning pulse is sent to the leyline nexus.
- Relay Blast:
  - burns out Mirrordune's command hardware,
  - sends a visible pulse through antenna fins or mirrored dunes,
  - may create a damaging shockwave if the party is close,
  - and erases most easy signal evidence at the site.
- Warning Pulse To The Nexus:
  - tells the nexus operators the relay chain is compromised,
  - orders them to destroy their command bolt,
  - orders them to destroy or purge their stupor-field device,
  - and likely causes them to flee, hide, or activate their own containment protocol.
- Result: Mirrordune may not give the party a clean fight, but it creates a clear consequence: the nexus is warned and starts destroying evidence.

- The command bolt at the site has been shattered or melted.
- The stupor-field device has been cracked, purged, or partly dumped into a leyline vent.
- Several crystal cradles are empty but still glowing with residual charge.
- One or two damaged refined cores remain because they were too unstable to load safely.
- A half-filled crate of uncharged but refined crystals remains near the loading area. The charged shipment escaped by vehicle, but this unfinished feedstock was abandoned when the warning and purge sequence interrupted the final transfer.
- Vehicle tracks lead away from the nexus toward [[Heliox Prime]].
- A five-seat sand buggy sits half-destroyed near the loading area or tucked behind a rock shelf. The same cleanup order that purged the stupor-field device also damaged the buggy so the workers could not simply leave or be traced cleanly back to Heliox.
- The four Heliox workers used the buggy to get here from Heliox, likely with one Conclave handler, supervisor, or controlled driver.
- A circular patch of fused stone, star-cold ash, or folded sand marks where the Conclave escape portal opened.
- Conclave tools, burned calibration strips, or a dropped veil token show that the people running the site were not ordinary Heliox workers.
- The site contains signs of controlled labor:
  - clean workstations with no personal effects,
  - repeated tool placement,
  - command marks etched near work bays,
  - and workers' logs that read like compliance reports instead of normal shift notes.
- If any workers remain, they are confused, frightened, or coming out of the stupor field rather than eager to fight.
- Four Heliox workers remain unconscious when the party arrives. They were likely caught in the collapse of the stupor-field device or abandoned because they were not portable evidence.

### Useful Evidence
- Residue from the destroyed command bolt proves the nexus was receiving orders through the same family of command hardware as the Reclaimer.
- The broken stupor-field device proves the Conclave was not merely stealing crystals; it was controlling or suppressing workers at the charging site.
- The charge cradles show that the Conclave needed refined crystals already processed elsewhere, tying back to Selix's memory of factory work.
- A damaged transit ledger may show count totals but not final destinations:
  - `12` charged cores prepared,
  - `10` transferred,
  - `2` unstable or abandoned.
- A leyline stress pattern suggests the charging process is not natural Heliox practice. It is forcing the nexus harder than it was meant to be used.

### Four Unconscious Heliox Workers
- These workers are victims and witnesses, not enemies.
- They are unconscious when found, with no active command bolt visible.
- Their tools, station marks, or chest plates identify them as Heliox-affiliated industrial or refinement workers.
- Their half-destroyed five-seat sand buggy confirms they came from [[Heliox Prime]] by ground travel rather than portal.
- They may have been controlled through the local stupor-field device rather than implanted sleeper devices.
- They wake slowly if treated, stabilized, or removed from the lingering field residue.
- Initial memory state:
  - remember reporting for a normal refinement or recharge assignment,
  - remember a shift supervisor or dispatch order that felt routine,
  - then lose time just like Selix and Varyn,
  - do not remember agreeing to Conclave work,
  - do not remember charging the crystals,
  - do not remember the final crystal shipment,
  - may recall a repeated phrase from the [[Civic Directive Lattice]].
- Possible names:
  - Dhoral-5, charge cradle technician,
  - Merix-7, leyline pressure reader,
  - Halix-10, refinement-loader,
  - Veyra-9, safety scribe.
- What they can provide after waking:
  - confirmation that the site was presented as official Heliox work,
  - confirmation that they rode out from Heliox in the sand buggy before losing time,
  - a repeated dispatch phrase that matches Heliox civic command cadence,
  - recognition of Selix's factory or refinement process if Selix is present,
  - no memory of the controlled period, matching the earlier survivors,
  - and emotional proof that Heliox workers are being used rather than simply collaborating.
- What they cannot provide:
  - the identity of the Conclave handler,
  - the full destination of the charged crystals,
  - or a clean memory of the controlled period.

## Sovereign Discovery At The Leyline Nexus
This is a good place to give the construct PC a meaningful discovery without solving the whole Sovereign mystery.

### Core Idea
- The leyline nexus was not originally a Conclave site.
- It is built on older Sovereign-era or Accord-era infrastructure designed to regulate safe crystal recharge.
- The Conclave is forcing the old system into a faster, harsher charge cycle.
- The construct PC may recognize the architecture not as memory exactly, but as authority responding to them.

### What The Sovereign Might Discover
- The nexus was built to slow and regulate leyline flow, not intensify it.
- The original system was designed with refusal-like safeguards: it could deny unsafe charge requests.
- The Conclave bypassed those safeguards by using obedience-style command logic on workers and crude override shims on the machinery.
- The construct PC's presence may cause one old regulator to wake, pause, or ask for authorization.
- The regulator does not say "Sovereign" aloud unless you want a big reveal. It might instead display an old designation:
  - `ACCORD EXECUTIVE PROFILE DETECTED`
  - `RECHARGE SAFEGUARD AWAITING AUTHORITY`
  - `OVERRIDE CHAIN COMPROMISED`

### Possible Sovereign Interface Benefits
- Identify that the Conclave is overcharging crystals beyond safe tolerances.
- Stabilize one abandoned refined core so it can be moved safely.
- Restore a fragmentary memory of the Sovereign designing or approving safe recharge limits.
- Shut down a dangerous leyline surge without damaging the nexus.
- Learn that the nexus is one small part of a larger recharge network tied to the [[Elemental Crucible]].

### Possible Sovereign Memory Fragment
You remember standing in a chamber like this before it had a name. Not this exact room, perhaps, but this kind of room: stone ribs around a quiet line of light, crystal cradles waiting empty, every gauge built to slow the hunger of desperate hands. Someone beside you asked why the system should be allowed to refuse a charge request. You answered without hesitation: because survival without limits becomes extraction.

### What Not To Reveal Yet
- Do not reveal the full location or function of the [[Elemental Crucible]].
- Do not explain the entire Sovereign memory loss.
- Do not prove exactly who inside Heliox gave the Conclave access.
- Do not reveal the final destination of all charged crystals unless you want to accelerate the larger Conclave plot.

## If The Party Reaches Heliox
Use this if the party follows the relay chain, the leyline nexus trail, or the broader command evidence toward [[Heliox Prime]].

### Approach From Mirrordune
- The party arrives with proof of a three-relay field network and possibly evidence of the leyline nexus.
- Heliox patrols and civic systems may still appear normal.
- The first unsettling thing should be how ordinary everything sounds:
  - public dispatches are calm,
  - patrol responses are precise,
  - workers follow normal routing,
  - and no one admits there is an emergency.
- The detector does not point to one villain tower. It catches the same cadence in many official messages.
- This suggests the Conclave is hiding inside normal civic command traffic.

### Approach From The Leyline Nexus
- The party arrives with evidence that Heliox-linked refined crystals are being charged off-site and moved by vehicle back toward Heliox.
- Selix's memory of factory work becomes more important.
- The trail may point toward:
  - an energy-core refinement district,
  - a civic dispatch routing office,
  - a crystal logistics annex,
  - or a maintenance authority tied to the [[Civic Directive Lattice]].
- Heliox does not look invaded. It looks scheduled.
- Workers near the relevant district may repeat phrases from public dispatches without noticing.

### First Heliox Target - Civic Directive Lattice
- The [[Civic Directive Lattice]] is the official Heliox command-and-dispatch network.
- Normal Purpose:
  - route work assignments,
  - coordinate patrols,
  - manage industrial shift timing,
  - issue preservation directives,
  - and keep energy rationing orderly.
- Conclave Abuse:
  - commands are hidden inside official dispatch cadence,
  - stupor-field effects make affected warforged receptive,
  - command bolts or sleeper devices carry direct instructions,
  - and civic messages make coercion look like normal procedure.
- The horror is not that Heliox is chaotic. The horror is that it is too orderly.

### What They Might Find In Heliox
- A public dispatch tower repeating a harmless civic phrase with the same cadence as Mirrordune.
- A worker leaving a district boundary who suddenly becomes confused before a supervisor redirects them back inside.
- A refinement shift log showing Selix or people like Selix reassigned without normal authorization.
- Maintenance records for "signal smoothing" or "dispatch harmonization" installed shortly before the Reclaimer incident.
- A patrol that treats the party's evidence as a procedural anomaly, not a crisis.
- A civic access panel with Conclave-style calibration shims hidden under Heliox casing.
- A list of missing, reassigned, or "quieted" workers from energy-core refinement sites.

### Heliox Scene Tone
- Avoid making every Heliox citizen hostile.
- Many are victims, unaware participants, or people whose normal beliefs are being exploited.
- Heliox ideology should still feel real: preservation, scarcity, and discipline matter to them.
- The Conclave's control should feel like a hand using that ideology as a glove.

### Heliox Complications
- If the party arrives openly with [[The Calyxis Reclaimer]], Heliox may read it as hostile military provocation.
- If they arrive quietly, they have less authority but a better chance to observe the control system.
- If Captain Talis or Calyxis soldiers are present, Heliox patrols may escalate faster.
- If the party brings Varyn or Selix, they may recognize dispatch cadences, factory marks, or assignment language.
- If the construct PC interfaces with the Lattice, the Conclave may detect a Sovereign-level profile.

### Heliox Leads Forward
- Find the local Lattice access point used to hide command inserts.
- Locate the energy-core refinement site tied to Selix's memory.
- Identify which civic office authorized the reassignment of controlled workers.
- Determine whether Heliox leaders are controlled, deceived, compartmentalized, or resisting.
- Trace where the charged crystals from the leyline nexus were sent.
