# Shattered Gears of Asmar

## Summary
The party arrives on [[Asmar]] just as its surviving construct cities balance on the edge of renewed war. After warning [[Calyxis Forge]] that the [[Astral Conclave]] intends to steal the continent's crystal energy supply, the players are drawn into mine defense, truce-breaking sabotage, and the return of a construct whose forgotten identity may decide the fate of the [[Elemental Crucible]].

## Objective
- Earn the trust of Calyxis Forge
- Defend the High Spine Mine without triggering open war
- Determine why [[Heliox Prime]] is willing to destroy infrastructure rather than let Calyxis continue extraction
- Follow Sovereign-era access points into the deeper truth of Asmar's failing energy system

## Key NPCs
- [[Magistra Pyra-IX]]
- [[Arch-Calculant Veyrix]]
- Warden-Guide Seris-9

## Locations
- [[Asmar]]
- [[Calyxis Forge]]
- [[Heliox Prime]]
- [[The Hollow Veins]]

## Obstacles
- The cold truce makes obvious alliances politically dangerous
- The mines are already strained by weakening crystal output
- Heliox sabotage units target stabilizers and extraction machinery instead of soldiers
- Heliox mobile raids can turn crystal theft into a moving battlefield before either city can admit what is happening publicly
- The [[Astral Conclave]] may intervene once both cities are distracted

## Twists
- The construct PC carries a pre-partition executive resonance signature tied to the Sovereign of Accord
- Hidden Crucible interfaces respond to resonance rather than remembered authority
- The real crisis may stem from failing planetary ley flow, not simple civic mismanagement

## Outcomes
- The party keeps the truce intact while exposing deeper truths
- The party helps one city at the cost of the other's trust
- The party uncovers evidence that Asmar's crisis is tied to the wider planar damage affecting the campaign
- The party triggers a premature struggle over control of the Crucible

## Open Questions
- What destroyed or sealed the Sovereign's memory after leaving Asmar?
- Can Asmar be saved without choosing between brutal scarcity and reckless extraction?
