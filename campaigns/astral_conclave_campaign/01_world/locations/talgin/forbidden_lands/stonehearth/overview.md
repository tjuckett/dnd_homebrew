# Stonehearth

## Summary
Stonehearth is an ancient earth elemental village hidden within a vast underground cavern in the [[Forbidden Lands]] of [[Talgin]]. Warm amber light from glowing crystals embedded in the cavern walls illuminates a settlement built from naturally formed stone dwellings, crystal-worked ritual spaces, and enduring communal structures.

## Purpose
Stonehearth serves as a stable elemental community shaped around endurance, ritual, craft, and mutual support. It is both a refuge for earth elementals and a cultural center where practical survival and spiritual continuity remain tightly linked.

## Tone
Ancient, grounded, and quietly sacred. Stonehearth feels patient rather than stagnant, with every structure and custom shaped by long memory and careful stewardship.

## Features
- Naturally formed stone huts and chambers that blend seamlessly into the cavern
- Crystal decorations and gemstone inlays attuned to individual elemental resonance
- The [[Heartstone Altar]], a massive glowing crystal at the village center used for major rites
- Crystal Gardens cultivated for beauty, energy, and communal sustenance
- The Great Forge, where durable tools, weapons, and stonecraft are created
- Healing Springs infused with restorative elemental energy

## Defenses
- The village's hidden underground setting limits casual intrusion
- Earth elemental defenders know the cavern terrain intimately
- Strong communal response under the leadership of [[Granitefist]]
- Durable stone architecture and controlled access points make direct assault difficult

## Society
- Around 100 earth elementals live in the village
- Common roles include warriors, healers, miners, crafters, and ritualists
- Daily life centers on mining, crystal cultivation, stonecraft, healing work, storytelling, and rites honoring the earth
- Trusted trade partners may exchange rare goods for minerals, gemstones, healing crystals, crafted stone tools, and elemental essences

## Lifestyle
- The village's dwellings are not sharply separated from the cavern, but shaped to feel like a continuation of the earth itself
- Communal gatherings are central to village life, with stories, shared labor, and ritual carrying as much importance as defense or craft
- Crystal farms and mineral work are practical necessities as well as spiritual expressions of harmony with the land
- The village values durability, continuity, and resonance over speed, novelty, or expansion

## Possessions and Trade
- Precious minerals and gemstones used for both ornament and practical craft
- Elemental essences kept for rituals, healing, and specialized creation
- Stonecrafted tools and weapons shaped as both art and utility
- Healing crystals prepared for restorative use
- Trade is cautious and limited to trusted outsiders rather than open commerce

## Notable Areas
- [[Heartstone Altar]]: Sacred center of village ritual life and major ceremonies
- Crystal Gardens: Carefully tended luminous growths that provide beauty, energy, and a sense of communal stability
- Great Forge: A large forge empowered by earth-aspected energy and used for unusually durable works
- Healing Springs: Underground restorative waters maintained through ritual care and close watch

## Hooks
- Diplomatic relations, trade, or alliance-building with elemental leaders
- Ritual participation at the [[Heartstone Altar]] in exchange for blessings or aid
- Defensive missions to protect the village or surrounding elemental lands
- Healing and purification work tied to corruption spreading through nearby regions
- Resource-gathering or crafting-focused quests involving crystals, essences, or forge-work

## Secrets
- Stonehearth's continued survival depends on careful trust; not every outsider is permitted to see the village's resources or rituals.
- Its stores of elemental essence, healing crystals, and crafted stonework make it valuable to any force willing to exploit elemental communities.
