# Kira's Abandoned Workshop

## Summary
[[Kira's Abandoned Workshop]] is one of [[Kira]]'s early boundary-workshop sites hidden deep in the [[Forbidden Lands]]. Built as part of the old workshop network that helped keep elementals from crossing freely into humanoid lands, it now stands dormant, half-sealed, and dangerous to anyone who mistakes it for a simple ruin.

## Purpose
This site gives the campaign a grounded example of Kira's older legacy before her retreat to [[Kira's Hidden Lab]]: a morally compromised field installation where protection, containment, and exploitation were fused together.

## Tone
Buried, unstable, and secretive. The workshop feels like a place that once ran with relentless purpose and was then abandoned in haste after its creator decided it could no longer be trusted.

## History
- The workshop was built after the great elemental catastrophes as one node in Kira's distributed boundary network.
- Its machinery was designed to attract, bind, and drain elemental power into an orb-driven containment system.
- The surrounding boundary effects helped reduce elemental movement into nearby humanoid lands, which is one reason later generations came to think the region's danger had natural limits.
- Kira eventually abandoned the site rather than continue maintaining it openly, leaving behind wards, encoded notes, and automated defenses.
- Unlike [[Kira's Hidden Lab]], this site was purpose-built by Kira herself rather than reclaimed from older infrastructure.

## Structure
- Concealed Entrance: A collapsed ravine path and hidden stone door masked by old illusion work and weathered ward-lines.
- Intake Hall: Sensor pylons, containment rails, and ruined machinery once used to pull or direct elemental movement.
- Orb Chamber: The core room where a bound elemental orb powered the local node and synchronized with the wider workshop network.
- Map Wall: A sealed wall that can reveal hidden markings, workshop routes, or encoded notes when illuminated by an [[Everburning Candle]] or similar Kira-linked light source.
- Guardian Bays: Recessed alcoves where dormant construct guardians or beetle-like maintenance units may still activate if the site is disturbed.

## Defenses
- Illusion masking and misdirection wards designed to hide the workshop from casual discovery
- Old construct guardians that may still respond to intrusion or tampering
- Failing containment fields that create intermittent elemental surges, unstable terrain, or environmental hazards
- Access logic keyed to Kira's old command patterns, making some systems difficult to interpret without notes, relics, or experimentation

## Discoveries
- Evidence that the workshop network helped protect nearby lands, but only by trapping and draining elemental prisoners
- Logs, diagrams, and maintenance fragments pointing toward the wider [[Workshop Network]]
- Hidden map data that can expose additional workshop locations or relay paths
- Signs that Kira came to distrust her own system and began hiding, fragmenting, or sabotaging parts of it before she vanished from the public world

## Secrets
- The workshop is not merely abandoned; parts of it were deliberately left difficult to restart because Kira feared the system being misused.
- Disabling the site may free trapped elemental power while also weakening whatever remains of the local boundary effect.
- Some of the oldest machinery may incorporate adapted knowledge derived from the same post-war crisis that later produced the corruption around [[Valtoria]] and [[The Technomire]].
