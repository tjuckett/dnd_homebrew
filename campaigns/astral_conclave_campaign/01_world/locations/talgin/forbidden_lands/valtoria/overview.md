# Valtoria

## Summary
Valtoria, now remembered as the Lost City, was once a grand metropolis built by an ancient civilization that blended technology with elemental magic at extraordinary scale. Now buried within the [[Forbidden Lands]] of [[Talgin]], its towering spires, engineered waterways, statues, and elemental infrastructure survive only in ruin and memory.

## Purpose
Valtoria stands as the ruined historical core of [[The Technomire]] and one of the campaign's clearest examples of how ambitious elemental engineering can collapse into long-term environmental catastrophe. It is a place of buried knowledge, corrupted infrastructure, and unresolved causes.

## Tone
Majestic in ruin, toxic in practice, and haunted by failed brilliance. The city feels like a dead age still trying to function through broken systems.

## History
- Valtoria flourished during the height of an ancient civilization skilled in elemental magitech
- Its prosperity centered on the Elemental Nexus, a vast energy hub that powered industry, homes, and stabilizing systems throughout the city
- Roughly 200 years ago, after the Great Elemental War, the city suffered a catastrophic collapse when the Nexus malfunctioned or was tampered with
- Surviving emergency archives indicate corrupted or compelled elementals were drawn toward the failing Nexus during the disaster rather than acting as simple outside invaders
- Uncontrolled elemental energy tore through the city, split the earth, poisoned waterways, and drove survivors to flee or perish
- Over time, the surrounding ruin decayed into the poisoned swamp now known as [[The Technomire]]

## What Remains
- The Central Plaza, now shattered around the corrupted remains of the Elemental Nexus
- The Grand Library, partially standing and potentially still holding intact records
- The Machinery District, filled with unstable constructs, ruined devices, and dangerous energy surges
- The Subterranean Tunnels, a humming maze of buried infrastructure beneath the city
- Broken monuments, flooded streets, and overgrown districts warped by corruption and time

## The City Today
- Much of the city has been swallowed by the swamp that became [[The Technomire]]
- Spires and public works lie collapsed, flooded, or half-submerged in poisoned growth
- Poisonous waters and unstable energies prevent the land from truly healing around the ruins
- The city remains valuable because it still contains relics, records, and ancient systems beneath the decay

## District Details
- Central Plaza: Broken pillars, shattered fountains, and the corrupted remains of the Nexus dominate the city's symbolic center
- Grand Library: Overrun by vines and hostile growth, but still one of the best places to recover surviving records
- Machinery District: Home to unstable constructs, erratic fields, and the remnants of the city's technological marvels
- Subterranean Tunnels: A dangerous lower maze that once supported the Nexus and still hums with old power

## Dangers
- Noxious floodwaters and sludge-choked streets
- Corrupted elemental surges that distort magic and terrain
- Twisted creatures and hostile constructs shaped by Nexus fallout
- Structural collapse, hidden tunnels, and unstable ruins

## Exploration Use
- An ideal site for recovery of lost technology, maps, and elemental theory
- A dangerous place where every discovery risks awakening something preserved badly
- A strong source of atmosphere for themes of hubris, decay, and unintended inheritance

## Secrets
- The exact trigger of Valtoria's fall remains unknown, but archive fragments from High Engineer Lira Destra show the Nexus was already unstable before the final evacuation.
- Those same fragments suggest the Nexus attracted and corrupted nearby elementals as it spiraled, helping turn a systems failure into a city-killing catastrophe.
- Kira Emberweaver's later prototype work appears to have interacted with, repurposed, or further destabilized the already-corrupted systems at the city's core.
- The city may still contain records explaining how ancient elemental infrastructure succeeded before it failed.

## Quest Use
- Deep exploration site within [[Swamp Corruption]]
- Source of relics, lost records, and prototype evidence
- Potential final approach to the corrupted core driving the wider mire
