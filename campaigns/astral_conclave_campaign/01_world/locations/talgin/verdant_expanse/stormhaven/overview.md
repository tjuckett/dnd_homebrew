# Stormhaven

## Summary
Stormhaven is a smaller, run-down coastal town in the temperate reaches of [[The Verdant Expanse]], situated along an important but neglected trade route. Once more prosperous, it now survives in a state of patched-together decline under the control of the [[Shadow Fangs]], who use it as both a covert stronghold and a source of ongoing leverage.

## Purpose
Stormhaven serves as a compromised port town where decayed civic life, criminal rule, and covert Conclave activity overlap. It is a strong setting for intrigue, rebellion, disappearances, illicit trade, and moral compromise under occupation.

## Tone
Criminal, paranoid, and worn thin. Survival continues here, but almost every form of stability is compromised by extortion, secrecy, or quiet fear.

## Shadow Fang Control
- The [[Shadow Fangs]] seized Stormhaven not as conquerors seeking spectacle, but as opportunists securing a durable foothold
- They maintain control through intimidation, selective tolerance, and economic dependence rather than constant open violence
- Commerce is allowed to continue so long as the Fangs can tax it, exploit it, or use it
- Their hidden stronghold in town lets them influence the coast while staying partly obscured from wider scrutiny

## Agreement with the Astral Conclave
- The [[Astral Conclave]] provides the Shadow Fangs with pre-war weapons, tools, and arcane devices
- In return, the Conclave maintains a hidden portal beneath Stormhaven
- The portal enables covert travel, mercenary movement, and deeper experimentation away from public oversight
- The Conclave can also call directly on Shadow Fang assistance when needed, binding the relationship through mutual usefulness rather than trust

## Appearance and Atmosphere
- Cobblestone streets are cracked, narrow, and littered with signs of neglect
- Buildings lean, rot, or stand patched together from better years
- The harbor still functions, but its piers and vessels look battered and undermaintained
- Ruined towers and remnants of arcane infrastructure hint at pre-war prosperity beneath present decay
- The townsfolk live under a persistent atmosphere of unease, where disappearances and rumors travel quietly but constantly

## First View
- Approaching the town, the smell of salt and sea shifts into rot, smoke, and quiet oppression
- The docks reach into the bay like skeletal fingers, with patched ships and torn sails swaying in bitter wind
- Faded Shadow Fang banners mark the town's control openly enough to warn, but not enough to reassure
- Enforcers patrol the square with humming devices that silence conflict before it grows
- Even in decline, flashes of resilience remain in places like the Temple of Tides and Joren Thatch's forge

## Key Locations
- The Docks: Main center of trade, taxation, and covert movement, with hidden smuggler's coves below
- The Shadow Fang Citadel: Repurposed mayoral mansion that now serves as Kaelric's seat of power, including a grounded war room for local planning and crisis response
- The Portal Caverns: Hidden underground site containing the Conclave's portal and nearby laboratories
- The Rusted Anchor Inn: Tavern and inn near the harbor used for drink, rumor, and clandestine meetings
- Joren's Forge: Smithy blending old methods with salvaged pre-war technology, secretly aiding the rebellion
- The Broken Compass: General store dealing in supplies, salvage, and restricted backroom trade
- The Temple of Tides: Crumbling sanctuary and refuge for the needy, quietly tied to resistance
- The Market Square: Half-strangled commercial center operating under oppressive taxation
- Lysara's Den: Hidden espionage hub connected to intelligence gathering and covert routes
- The Wreckyard: Salvage ground where discarded machinery may still conceal valuable relics

## Portal Access
- The portal lies in the largest of the caverns beneath town, set within a mechanical frame of uncertain origin
- Access routes include the Citadel's dungeons, a hidden dockside warehouse entrance, and a treacherous smuggler's tunnel
- Wards, constructs, checkpoints, and insignia-based controls make direct approach dangerous
- Nearby reclamation nodes and laboratories tie the portal to ongoing experimentation

## Conclave Machinery and Augmentations
- Aether rifles, pulse gauntlets, kinetic shields, and augmented limbs support Shadow Fang enforcement
- Arcane and neural implants have reportedly been used on enforcers and unsuspecting townsfolk alike
- Construct guardians, surveillance drones, temporal stabilizers, and energy reclamation nodes all suggest the town is more than a simple criminal port

## Tensions
- Shadow Fang authority depends on keeping the town weak enough to control but functional enough to exploit
- The rebellion lacks the resources to openly challenge Conclave-backed enforcers without outside help
- Townsfolk may fear both the Fangs and the consequences of resistance
- The Conclave's hidden experiments risk turning Stormhaven from a controlled asset into a planar disaster site
- Some disappearances begin with offers of medical aid, paid work, or relief, making desperation itself one of the Conclave's tools

## Secrets
- The arrangement between the Shadow Fangs and the [[Astral Conclave]] goes far beyond routine smuggling or black-market trade.
- Stormhaven is not only exploited for profit; it is being used as a covert site for augmentation, control, and planar experimentation.
- Not every victim is taken by force; the Conclave has used false generosity and promises of recovery or employment to draw vulnerable townsfolk into experimentation.
