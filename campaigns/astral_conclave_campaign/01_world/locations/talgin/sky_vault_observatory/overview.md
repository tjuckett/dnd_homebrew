# Sky Vault Observatory

## Summary
The Sky Vault Observatory is a shattered mountaintop ruin in [[Talgin]] where a once-magnificent floating fortress of glass, silver, and starlight crashed into a jagged plateau. Though its dome is broken, its towers lean at impossible angles, and parts of the structure still defy gravity, a preserved vault beneath the mountain remains active and dangerously intact.

## Purpose
The observatory serves as a pre-war site of celestial study, arcane navigation, and sealed knowledge. It is both a striking exploration location and a contained dungeon whose surviving inner systems still test intruders through sequence-based teleportation, false paths, and ancient defenses.

## Tone
Wind-scoured, luminous, and uncanny. The ruin above feels dead but not empty, while the vault below feels preserved, watchful, and unwilling to surrender its secrets.

## Features
- A broken silver-and-glass dome scattered across the cliffs
- Tilted towers, some still suspended by failing enchantments
- A cracked courtyard engraved with faded star maps that still pulse with residual magic
- A half-collapsed stair descending into a preserved inner complex
- Four humming teleportation circles in the Entrance Chamber of the sealed vault
- Strange atmospheric lights above the ruin, like stars flickering too close to the world

## Arrival
- The observatory is reached on a jagged mountaintop plateau where the air is thin and sharp
- Wind howls constantly across the cliffs, carrying the smell of stone and ozone
- The ruin first appears as the shattered carcass of a floating fortress rather than a conventional building
- The mountain itself seems to pull visitors downward toward the living vault hidden beneath the wreckage

## Ruins Above
- A ruined Celestial Library with scraps of star charts, research notes, comet logs, and scholar disputes
- Cracked observation decks with warped lenses and dangerous but useful views across the surrounding land
- A ruined Grand Orrery Chamber where broken crystal planets still drift in unstable motion
- Astronomers' quarters filled with journals, interrupted notes, and signs of sudden disaster
- Lookout towers that offer clear sightlines at the cost of collapse risk and brutal winds
- Mechanical rooms containing fractured levitation engines, unstable runes, and clues to the observatory's fall

## The Vault Below
The lower vault remains pristine compared to the ruin above. Its silverstone halls, glowing constellations, and teleportation circles still function as intended, forcing intruders to navigate a correct sequence of rooms while trap paths loop, punish, and reset progress.

## Descent
- The stairwell descends away from the winds into stillness and unnatural silence
- Smooth silverstone walls are carved with faintly glowing constellations
- The air grows cool, metallic, and charged with old arcane force
- The preserved lower chamber feels less ruined than waiting

## Rooms
- Entrance Chamber: Circular silverstone hall with four teleport circles and drifting constellations on the walls
- Star Chamber: Domed chamber with a living night sky reflected above and below
- Mirror Hall: Frosted mirrored room where reflections lag and whisper
- Gravity Well: Arcane chamber where gravity surges and debris shifts unpredictably
- Frozen Time Chamber: Hall of suspended clocks, halted sand, and broken temporal rhythm
- Astral Orrery: Cathedral-like chamber of damaged crystal planets and grinding celestial rings
- Celestial Vault: Final spherical chamber containing the vault's preserved prize and last guardian

## Correct Route Rooms
- Entrance Chamber: North -> Star Chamber
- Star Chamber: East -> Mirror Hall
- Mirror Hall: South -> Gravity Well
- Gravity Well: West -> Frozen Time Chamber
- Frozen Time Chamber: South -> Astral Orrery
- Astral Orrery: East -> Celestial Vault
- Celestial Vault: Stable exit returns the party to the mountain exterior after success

## Correct Route
- Entrance Chamber -> Star Chamber
- Star Chamber -> Mirror Hall
- Mirror Hall -> Gravity Well
- Gravity Well -> Frozen Time Chamber
- Frozen Time Chamber -> Astral Orrery
- Astral Orrery -> Celestial Vault

## Vault Logic
- Each correct room contains a true celestial or environmental clue pointing to the next exit
- Failed interpretation leads to a themed trap room rather than random navigation
- Returning to the Entrance Chamber resets sequence progress
- The final chamber is guarded and only opens a stable exit after the vault is successfully cleared or bypassed

## Trap Rooms
- Room of Chains: Animated chains lash and restrain intruders until the room is broken, burned through, or escaped correctly
- Firestorm Hall: Periodic flame jets and a hidden cold-based solution punish wrong movement
- Inverted Chamber: Gravity flips the room and turns movement into a timing puzzle
- Maze of Reflections: Mirror doubles and false selves force the party to break the illusion or fight through it
- Room of the Dead: Ghostly scholars present riddles backed by necrotic punishment or spectral attack
- Obsidian Hall: Light-drinking darkness, false acoustics, and pit traps conceal the route
- Void Chamber: A star-void isolates one target while the rest survive pressure and pull
- Starfire Trap Room: Rotating radiant beams sweep the room from a crystal overhead
- Room of the Astral Watcher: A construct guardian can be fought directly or disabled by targeting runes
- Collapsing Chamber: Repeating cave-ins force players to read safe zones under pressure
- Endless Labyrinth: Nearly identical loops hide subtle anomalies that must be recognized to escape
- Mirror of Past Selves: A malignant mirror traps attention and drains will unless shattered
- Frozen Wastes: Exhausting cold and ice conceal the mechanism that reveals the proper exit
- Chamber of Crushing Pressure: Magical force pins intruders down until vents or runes are disrupted
- Sandstorm Room: A field of sinking dust buries the party unless they reach stabilizing runes or create footing
- Needle Hall: Needle fire punishes hesitation in a narrow corridor
- Blade Corridor: Timed scythe-blades demand pattern-reading, jamming, or brute survival
- Silent Death Room: Poisonous vapor slowly overwhelms anyone who cannot vent or block the chamber
- Hall of Crushing Walls: Converging walls create a short clock to disable the mechanism
- Explosive Glyph Room: Active sigils detonate underfoot unless the safe path is read or suppressed
- Hall of False Vaults: Mimic-like false doors guard a reset circle behind deceptive grandeur

## Navigation Notes
- Each correct room contains a real clue and a convincing mislead
- Failed interpretation sends the party to a specific trap room rather than a random punishment
- Many trap rooms can be solved through more than one method, not only combat
- Even successful progression is meant to cost time, spells, hit points, or confidence

## Other Ruined Areas
- Celestial Library: Damaged star charts, comet logs, and scholar arguments survive in fragments
- Observation Decks: Collapsing balconies and warped lenses offer views, alignment clues, and eerie light effects
- Grand Orrery Chamber: Broken crystal planets still drift and may hint at vault sequencing if stabilized
- Astronomers' Quarters: Personal journals and abrupt final entries preserve traces of the last days
- Lookout Towers: High, unstable vantage points provide dangerous sightlines across the surrounding region
- Mechanical Rooms: Failed levitation engines and fractured runes may explain why the observatory fell

## Hazards
- Extreme altitude, unstable stone, and brutal mountain winds outside
- Gravity failures, residual star-magic, and arcane misfires within the ruin
- Trap chambers involving fire, void exposure, poison, crushing pressure, collapsing architecture, and hostile constructs
- The Starforged Sentinel or similar final guardian within the Celestial Vault

## Rewards
- Pre-war astronomical records and navigational data
- Names, arguments, and journals from scholars active before the observatory's fall
- Clues about comets, void anomalies, hidden routes, or other sky-linked sites
- A secured artifact, secret, or sealed truth within the Celestial Vault

## Secrets
- The ruin above crashed, but the deeper vault appears to have sealed itself and preserved its systems intentionally.
- The observatory likely fell during a moment of crisis rather than simple decay, and surviving engine rooms may still reveal why.
- The vault's sequence-based design suggests its keepers feared theft, misuse, or unworthy access more than outright destruction.
