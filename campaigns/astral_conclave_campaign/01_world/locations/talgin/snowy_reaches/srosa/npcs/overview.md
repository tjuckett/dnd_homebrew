# Srosa NPCs

## Summary
Srosa's residents reflect its frontier character: resilient, varied, and shaped by long years of surviving close to the Forbidden Lands.

## Residents
- Morgana Greenleaf: Elven botanist who tends village gardens, grows rare herbs, and prepares remedies with Serena's help
- Cedric Stonehelm: Dwarven brewmaster and publican whose ales and stories make him a cornerstone of tavern culture at the Hearthstone Haven
- Lydia Moonshadow: Halfling minstrel whose energy and music help define the town's public atmosphere
- Eldric Emberforge: Gnomish tinkerer known for whimsical inventions and practical repairs
- Serena Whitewind: Tiefling healer whose calm presence makes her one of the town's most trusted figures
- Gruff Stoneshield: Half-orc farmer whose strength and care for animals support the town's food supply
- Mirabelle Swiftfoot: Human librarian preserving local knowledge and feeding the town's curiosity
- Nia Starwhisper: Aarakocra watcher who keeps vigil over the settlement from above
- Eira Frostwind: Young tiefling orphan whose strange snow-drawn visions have become part of local rumor
- Eldra Thistlebrook: Halfling proprietor of the Rustic Provisions General Store, known for fair dealing and an excellent memory for who needs quiet help
- Rurik "Ironclad": Dwarven smith overseeing the Sturdy Steel Smithy, respected more for reliability than charm

## Resident Quirks
- Morgana speaks to plants as if they can answer in kind
- Cedric names every batch of ale after a local legend and insists on telling the tale
- Lydia performs with a songbird named Harmony woven into her music
- Eldric is accompanied by a mechanical squirrel named Sparky
- Serena keeps a collection of healing crystals in her office
- Gruff quietly adopts strays that wander onto his farm
- Mirabelle can recite the opening line of every book in the library from memory
- Nia communicates with locals through bird calls and hand-signals as often as speech
- Eira claims to see visions in the snow and sketches them in patterns some villagers treat as omens
- Eldra quietly keeps a shelf of unpaid essentials set aside for families having a bad winter
- Rurik tests the ring of every finished tool against his anvil before letting it leave the shop
