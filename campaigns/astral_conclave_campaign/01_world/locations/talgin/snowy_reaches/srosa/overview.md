# Srosa

## Summary
Srosa is a remote town in the [[Snowy Reaches]] on the northwestern side of [[Talgin]], founded in the aftermath of the Elemental Invasion when humanoid survivors fled to the continent and began rebuilding in scattered settlements. Perched along a meandering river and watched over by snow-capped mountains and an ancient forest, the town has endured for roughly fifty years despite its proximity to the edge of the Forbidden Lands.

## Purpose
Srosa represents one of Talgin's harder frontier settlements: a place where survival, beauty, and quiet dread coexist. It stands as both a successful post-invasion refuge and a reminder that peace on the border of elemental territory can never be fully trusted.

## Tone
Colorful, hardy, and uneasy beneath its comforts. Srosa looks warm and welcoming from a distance, but much of its stability rests on a mystery no one has solved and many older residents do not trust.

## Founding and History
- Srosa was established after the Elemental Invasion as part of the humanoid effort to rebuild on [[Talgin]]
- It has survived for about fifty years in a difficult but strategically defensible location
- For much of its early history, the town faced elemental attacks at least once each year
- Those attacks stopped roughly twenty years ago for reasons no one understands
- The halt in violence has made many younger townsfolk complacent, while older residents still fear that the quiet is temporary

## Setting
- The town lies beside a winding river that cuts through the settlement and helps define its daily life
- A wooden bridge spans the river and links the two halves of town
- Snow-capped mountains rise nearby, shaping the weather and framing the settlement
- An ancient forest stands close at hand, creating a natural border between the town and more dangerous lands
- Beyond the western edge lie the Forbidden Lands, where elementals still roam freely and few travelers return alive

## Appearance
- Small cottages line the riverbanks, their chimneys giving off steady smoke in the winter air
- Timbered homes and painted buildings create a colorful patchwork against the snow
- The village square acts as a festive central hub even during the coldest months
- Market stalls display winter fruits, handcrafted goods, and occasional magical trinkets
- Glowing windows, woodsmoke, and hearty meals give the town a strong sense of interior warmth despite the harsh surroundings

## Population and Character
- The town holds roughly 100 residents
- Its population is notably mixed across many humanoid ancestries
- Living in Srosa requires tolerance for cold, remoteness, and danger near the Forbidden Lands
- The town's culture has become visibly colorful and expressive in response to the harshness around it

## Defenses
- The river and bridge create natural movement bottlenecks
- The town once maintained a militia strong enough to answer regular elemental assaults
- Years without attacks have left that militia under-practiced and less ready than before
- Older residents still carry habits of vigilance even as the settlement itself has relaxed

## Daily Life
- Trade, farming, tavern life, small craftwork, and practical river use sustain the community
- Seasonal market activity remains important to morale as much as survival
- Residents live with a shared awareness that the frontier can turn dangerous quickly
- Daily life balances ordinary routines with inherited stories about the lands beyond town

## Tensions
- No one knows why the elemental attacks stopped
- The militia's decline leaves the town less prepared than its appearance suggests
- Generational differences shape how seriously the threat of renewed attack is taken
- Bordering the Forbidden Lands means every period of calm feels conditional

## Secrets
- Srosa's peace may be a pause rather than a victory.
- The town has slowly built its identity around surviving a danger it no longer actively confronts, which may leave it brittle if the old pattern returns.
