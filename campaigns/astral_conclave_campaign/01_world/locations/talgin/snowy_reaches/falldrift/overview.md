# Falldrift

## Summary
Falldrift is a small winter hamlet of roughly fifty residents in the [[Snowy Reaches]] of [[Talgin]], built along a cold river in a mountainous stretch of the continent. Though isolated and vulnerable, it endures through close communal ties, practical trade, and the stubborn resilience of people accustomed to long winters and uncertain borders.

## Purpose
Falldrift represents the kind of fragile but functioning settlement that defines much of post-invasion life on [[Talgin]]: small, self-reliant, and close enough to danger that survival depends on cooperation more than strength.

## Tone
Quiet, hardy, and welcoming. Falldrift feels like a refuge held together by habit, labor, and shared winter rituals rather than by wealth or power.

## Setting
- The hamlet stands beside a swift icy river that shapes daily life and acts as a natural barrier
- A dense snow-laden forest lies beyond the settlement, marking the edge of lands locals consider dangerous or forbidden
- Mountains loom nearby, with rumors of hidden caves, buried treasures, and older dangers beneath the snow
- The central square holds a frozen fountain and a modest cluster of trade stalls
- Snow-capped peaks and deep winter weather shape nearly every practical choice in the settlement

## Daily Life
- Ice fishing, hunting, tailoring, smithing, and small-scale trade keep the settlement alive
- Villagers gather around shared hearths during the harshest weather
- The community relies on a mix of local labor and occasional goods brought in from elsewhere
- Elemental tales and warnings about the nearby wilds remain part of everyday life
