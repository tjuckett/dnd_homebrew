# Talgin

## Summary
One of the three main continents on Tertara, Talgin is the last known continent where humanoids still openly survive. After the elemental invasion, human survivors fled here and spread outward in scattered attempts to rebuild some form of civilization.

## Purpose
Talgin serves as the fractured heartland of post-invasion humanoid survival. It is less a unified realm than a wide patchwork of isolated villages, hard-won farmland, and defensive habits built around distance, caution, and endurance.

## Settlement Pattern
- Human survivors spread widely across the continent after the elemental invasion rather than rebuilding as a single state
- Small towns and villages survive far apart from one another
- Contact between settlements is infrequent enough that many people never travel beyond their birthplace
- Civilization on Talgin persists in fragments rather than through unified institutions

## Tone
Sparse, wary, and resilient. Life continues here, but rarely at scale. Most communities measure success by surviving another season, not by expanding their influence.

## Defenses
- Distance between settlements makes large-scale coordination rare, but it also limits how quickly threats spread from one town to another.
- Most villages are isolated enough that outside contact is uncommon and often treated with caution.
- Survival depends more on local self-reliance than on any continental authority.

## Secrets
- Talgin is populated, but not connected. Entire communities may know almost nothing about the state of the wider continent.
- A person can live and die in the village where they were born without ever meeting someone from another settlement.
- The apparent emptiness between settlements hides how many people survive in scattered pockets rather than organized nations.
