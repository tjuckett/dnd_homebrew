# Heliox Prime

## Summary
[[Heliox Prime]] is the eastern survivor-city of [[Asmar]], a monumental construct metropolis of brass towers, mirrored arrays, and tightly regulated continuity. It stands for the Preservation Doctrine: survival through limits, reduced consumption, and refusal to spend the last future the continent has left.

## Purpose
Heliox Prime gives the Asmar arc its strongest moral counterweight. It is not simply an enemy city, but a civilization willing to become smaller, colder, and harsher if that is the price of making Asmar last.

## Tone
Ordered, severe, and dignified. Heliox feels stable at first glance, but that stability is purchased through austerity, mandatory dormancy, and increasingly ruthless interventions.

## Leadership
- [[Arch-Calculant Veyrix]]: Mathematical ruler of Heliox Prime and chief voice of preservation through imposed limits

## Outlook
- Calyxis extraction is seen as a direct shortening of Asmar's life
- The truce is useful only so long as it prevents catastrophic escalation
- Sabotage can be justified if it prevents irreversible depletion
- The return of a pre-partition executive resonance profile is politically destabilizing, whether or not the construct PC remembers anything

## Defenses
- Solar mirror arrays that can redirect heat and light into controlled kill zones
- Rigid transit control, surveillance architecture, and locked civic routing
- Elite covert units trained to disable infrastructure rather than seek glorious battle
- Deep archival authority that can validate old executive signatures even when modern citizens do not understand them
- Mobile siege crawlers such as [[The Calyxis Reclaimer]] allow Heliox to strike crystal routes, mines, and convoy lines far from the city without committing to open conventional war

## Secrets
- Some Heliox officials suspect the Elemental Crucible's safeguards are deliberate and that Calyxis is misdiagnosing the crisis.
- Veyrix would rather commit covert acts of truce-breaking than allow Calyxis to prove expansion was necessary.
- Heliox likely holds partial records that identify the construct PC as the Sovereign of Accord, but they are too dangerous to disclose casually.
- Desert raids on crystal transport may be framed as preservationist necessity even when they risk pushing the truce into open collapse.
