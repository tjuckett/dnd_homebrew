# The Hollow Veins

## Summary
[[The Hollow Veins]] are a collapsed under-network of caves, mining scars, and Sovereign-era conduits beneath the deserts and mountain approaches of [[Asmar]]. They connect crystal extraction zones, sealed research chambers, and hidden [[Elemental Crucible]] interfaces.

## Purpose
The Hollow Veins are the ideal site for memory fragments, discovery, sabotage trails, and first proof that the Sovereign left behind access rather than armies. They let the party move between political conflict and deep-setting truth.

## Tone
Silent, unstable, and haunted by utility rather than ghosts. The Veins feel abandoned only until old systems wake.

## Features
- Half-buried transit passages and collapsed mining tunnels
- Flickering memory echoes stored in damaged pre-war crystal lattices
- Cracked conduits bleeding unstable elemental charge
- Sealed chambers that open only for the construct PC's resonance profile
- Older-than-war control dais structures whose purpose modern constructs only partly understand

## Defenses
- Environmental instability, cave-ins, and resonance surges
- Legacy locks that ignore modern city authority
- Old sentinel systems that may reactivate when Crucible interfaces are disturbed

## Secrets
- Some sealed chambers contain maps, notes, and partial research on "external elemental anchors" beyond Asmar.
- The deepest interfaces may reveal that the Crucible was never intended as a permanent answer, only a bridge.
- Conclave operatives would pay dearly for access to the Veins, especially if they learn the construct PC can open them.
