# Asmar

## Summary
[[Asmar]] is a desert continent of construct civilization beyond [[Talgin]], ringed by crystal-rich mountains and scarred by the long aftermath of the Elemental War. It was once unified under the Construct Accord and the leadership of the vanished Sovereign of Accord, but now survives under a cold civil truce between its last two great cities.

## Purpose
Asmar provides a campaign-scale setting for construct politics, energy scarcity, and the return of buried history. It expands the scope of the campaign beyond Talgin while tying the region directly back to planar instability, [[Kira]]'s research legacy, and the [[Astral Conclave]]'s hunger for dangerous power sources.

## History
- After the Construct Uprising and the Elemental War, constructs and humanoids forged the Construct Accord.
- Some older humanoid-era records refer to the first awakened constructs as "Harvesters," though the label now carries the tone of pre-Accord fear and propaganda more than neutral description.
- Under that peace, constructs remained on [[Asmar]] while humanoid survivors rebuilt on [[Talgin]].
- That peace only became possible after construct and humanoid survivors cooperated to close the planar breaches that had turned the wider war into civilizational collapse.
- The Harmonized Age followed, during which construct society developed philosophy, culture, infrastructure, and long-term civic purpose.
- The Sovereign of Accord left Asmar to seek a lasting solution to the continent's finite energy future and never returned.
- One century ago, dwindling crystal reserves and disagreement over how to survive sparked the civil war between [[Heliox Prime]] and [[Calyxis Forge]].
- Open war paused roughly six years ago, but the truce holds only because neither city can afford to break it first.

## The Energy Crisis
- The mountains hold ancient elemental crystals left behind by the war.
- Those crystals are powerful but finite and do not naturally regenerate on Asmar.
- The Sovereign designed the [[Elemental Crucible]], a hidden recharge network that slowly restores depleted crystals using ley line energy.
- The Crucible was deliberately locked so it could not drain the planet or pull directly from elemental planes.
- Recharge rates are now falling, likely because ley flow itself has weakened after being cut off from the elemental planes, though no one on Asmar knows that for certain.

## Current Powers
- [[Calyxis Forge]] argues that survival requires adaptation, continued mining, and new methods before the energy collapse becomes irreversible.
- [[Heliox Prime]] argues that survival requires limits, conservation, and harsh restraint before Calyxis burns through the continent's remaining future.
- The [[Astral Conclave]] sees Asmar's crystal reserves and Crucible-linked technology as exploitable assets rather than a civilization worth preserving.

## First Impressions
- Endless dunes broken by metallic debris and half-buried pre-war works
- Jagged mountain passes threaded with mines, pylons, and old transit routes
- Construct patrols that behave more like border officials than raiders
- Cities that feel ancient, functional, and emotionally distant until old systems react to the wrong person

## Secrets
- One of the player characters is almost certainly the vanished Sovereign of Accord, though their memory is fragmented or absent.
- The Sovereign did not leave commands behind; they left sealed access points, unfinished research, and infrastructure that responds to a resonance signature older than the war.
- The true long-term problem may not be mining alone, but the wider severing of planar energy flow across the world.
